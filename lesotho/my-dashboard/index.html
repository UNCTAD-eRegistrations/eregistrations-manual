1. What is My Dashboard?

My Dashboard is your personal home page on Lesotho's business registration portal at businessregistrations.gov.ls. Once you log in, the dashboard gives you a complete view of all your businesses and lets you take action â€” all in one place.

Think of it as a control room for your business life. Instead of visiting multiple government offices or digging through paper records, you can see the status of every business you own, check which licences are coming up for renewal, and submit applications online.

Who uses My Dashboard?

Any Mosotho citizen or legal person who has registered (or plans to register) a business in Lesotho. This includes:

  • Sole traders (individuals running a business under their own name or a business name)
  • Directors, shareholders, or officers of registered companies
  • Representatives acting on behalf of a business through delegated rights
Note: My Dashboard is not the same as the registration form. It is what you see after you have already created an account and logged in. If you have not yet registered an account, you will first be asked to create one.

2. Getting Started

What you need

  • A computer, tablet, or smartphone with an internet connection
  • A registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls
  • Your national ID number (for identity verification â€” see Section 3)

How to log in

  1. Open your browser and go to businessregistrations.gov.ls
  2. Click Log In or Sign In at the top-right of the page
  3. Enter your registered email address and password, then click Sign In
  4. If you have not yet created an account, click Create Account and follow the prompts
  5. Once signed in, you are taken directly to My Dashboard
Tip: If you forget your password, click Forgot Password on the login page. A reset link will be sent to your registered email address within a few minutes.

What happens automatically when you log in

The moment you land on My Dashboard, the system quietly fetches your information from government databases â€” you do not need to search or enter anything. It retrieves:

  • All sole proprietorships linked to your national ID
  • All companies you are associated with (as director, shareholder, or officer)
  • Active licences and business activities for each business
  • Your identity verification (KYC) status

This may take a few seconds. If the page shows a loading spinner, please wait â€” it is working.

3. Identity Verification (KYC)

Before you can use all features of My Dashboard, the system needs to confirm who you are. This process is called Know Your Customer (KYC). It links your portal account to your real identity using your Lesotho national ID.

KYC is a one-time step. Once approved, you will never need to do it again on this portal.

Why is KYC required?

Lesotho's business registration laws require that government knows the true identity of business owners. KYC prevents fraud, protects your businesses from being misused, and ensures that only you (or someone you authorise) can manage your registrations.

KYC Status

Your KYC status will be one of three things:

Status What it means What you should do

In Review You submitted your details and they are being checked by the registry Wait â€” this usually takes 1â€“2 business days. You will receive a notification.
Approved Your identity has been confirmed. Full dashboard access is unlocked. No action needed. Proceed to use your dashboard.

5. Managing Your Businesses

Each business card on My Dashboard has an Actions area with buttons for common tasks. The actions available depend on whether the business is a sole proprietorship or a company.

Sole Proprietorship actions

  • Update data
  • Renew Business
  • Delegate rights
  • Close business

Company actions

  • Update data
  • Annual filing
  • Delegate rights
  • Close business

Renew Your Business or Licence

Business registrations and licences in Lesotho must be renewed periodically â€” usually every year. The Renew Business button starts the renewal application for your sole proprietorship. Licences for individual activities renew at the same time.

You will know renewal is due because:

  • The Renewal date shown on your business card is approaching
  • Your business status may change from Active to Expired if the deadline passes
  • The portal may show a warning banner

How to renew

  1. On your business card, click Renew Business
  2. Review the pre-filled details. The system fills in most information automatically
  3. Confirm any changes if needed and attach required documents
  4. Pay the renewal fee online (if applicable)
  5. Submit â€” a new application will appear in My Applications
Renew before the deadline. Operating an expired business registration is a legal violation. Renewal applications submitted late may incur penalty fees. Check your renewal dates regularly and set a personal reminder a month before the deadline.

Update Business Data

If your business details change â€” for example, your trading address, phone number, or the nature of an activity â€” you must update the official record. Click Update data to open an amendment application.

Common reasons to update:

  • New business address or contact details
  • Change in business activities or scope
  • Change in ownership (for sole proprietorships)
  • Change in company directors or shareholders
  1. Click Update data on the relevant business card
  2. The current registered data loads automatically into the form
  3. Edit only the fields that need changing
  4. Attach any supporting documents (e.g. lease agreement for new address)
  5. Submit. The update appears in My Applications with status Submitted
Tip: Keep your contact details up to date. The registry uses your registered email and phone number to send important notices about your business, including renewal reminders and inspection notifications.

Delegate Rights to Someone Else

Delegating rights means authorising another person â€” such as an employee, accountant, or lawyer â€” to manage your business on this portal on your behalf. The delegate can perform actions you choose, without needing your login credentials.

This is useful when:

  • You are travelling and cannot access the portal
  • Your accountant handles your renewals and filings
  • You want an employee to update business data without full access

How to delegate

  1. Click Delegate rights on the relevant business card
  2. Enter the national ID number or email of the person you wish to authorise
  3. Select which rights to grant (e.g. renew only, or full management)
  4. Set an expiry date for the delegation if desired
  5. Confirm. The delegate receives an email notification
Warning: Only delegate to people you fully trust. A delegate can submit applications and make changes in your name. You remain legally responsible for all actions taken under your business registration. You can revoke delegation at any time through the same menu.

Close a Business

If you permanently stop operating a business, you must formally close (deregister) it. Simply stopping trading without deregistering means you may still owe annual fees and remain legally liable. Click Close business to begin the deregistration process.

This action is permanent. Closing a business cancels its registration and all associated licences. Make sure you have stopped all operations, settled outstanding taxes, and informed employees before proceeding. Once approved, deregistration cannot be reversed.
  1. Click Close business on the business card
  2. Read the confirmation screen carefully
  3. Confirm the reason for closure and the effective date
  4. Attach a tax clearance certificate if required
  5. Submit the closure application
  6. The Registrar's office reviews and approves the closure â€” you receive a confirmation

Annual Filing (Companies only)

All registered companies in Lesotho must submit an annual return (also called annual filing) to the Registrar of Companies each year. This confirms that the company is still active, its registered details are correct, and all directors and shareholders are up to date.

Failure to file on time results in:

  • Late filing penalties
  • The company being marked as non-compliant
  • Possible deregistration of the company by the Registrar
  1. Click Annual filing on the company card
  2. Review the pre-loaded company information (directors, shareholders, registered address)
  3. Confirm or update any details that have changed during the year
  4. Upload required financial statements if the company size requires them
  5. Pay the annual filing fee online
  6. Submit. You receive a filing confirmation receipt
Tip: Set a calendar reminder for the company's annual filing deadline. Your dashboard shows the Renewal date for each company â€” this is the date by which the annual filing must be completed.

8. Business Registries

The Business Registries tab provides public access to the official registries maintained by the Lesotho Registrar General. These are searchable databases of all legally registered entities in Lesotho â€” available to any member of the public.

Sole Proprietorships Registry

A public record of all registered sole proprietorships in Lesotho. You can search by business name or registration number to verify whether a business is legitimately registered. Useful when choosing a unique name before registering or checking a supplier's credentials.

Companies Registry

The official register of all incorporated companies in Lesotho. Shows company names, registration numbers, type of company, and registered address. Use this to verify a company's existence, check its status, or confirm its directors.

Beneficial Ownership Registry

A transparency register showing the real people (natural persons) who ultimately own or control companies registered in Lesotho. This registry supports Lesotho's anti-money-laundering and financial transparency obligations. It is separate from the shareholders register and identifies those with significant control or ownership of 25% or more.

Note: Searching these registries is free and does not require you to be logged in. The information shown is limited to what is publicly available by law. Some detailed documents (e.g. company constitutional documents) may require a formal request or a fee.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

What is KYC and why do I have to do it?

KYC stands for "Know Your Customer" â€” it is the process of confirming your identity before you can manage businesses on the portal. Lesotho law requires that the government knows who is behind every business registration to prevent fraud. You only need to do it once, and it usually takes 1â€“2 business days to be approved. Until it is approved, you can browse the portal but cannot take any actions on your businesses.

How do I renew my business registration or licence?

Go to the My Dashboard tab, find the business you want to renew, and click Renew Business. For companies, the equivalent is Annual filing. The system pre-fills most of the form â€” review it, make any corrections, pay the fee if required, and submit. Your renewal application will appear in My Applications and typically takes a few days to be processed.

My business does not appear on My Dashboard. What should I do?

First, make sure your KYC (identity verification) is Approved. If it is pending, complete that step first. If your KYC is approved but the business still does not appear, it may be registered under a different national ID number in the registry. Do not try to re-register â€” contact the Registrar General's office to request a linkage correction. Bring your original ID document and proof of business ownership.

Can I manage multiple businesses on one account?

Yes. My Dashboard is designed to show all businesses linked to your national ID number â€” there is no limit. Sole proprietorships and companies all appear on the same dashboard. If you are a director of several companies, they all appear in the Companies section. You can take actions on each business independently.

What does "Delegate rights" mean and is it safe?

Delegating rights means authorising another person (such as an accountant, lawyer, or trusted employee) to act on your behalf for a specific business on this portal. You choose what they can do and can set an expiry date. You remain legally responsible for all actions they take. Only delegate to people you fully trust, and revoke the delegation as soon as it is no longer needed. You can manage and revoke delegations at any time through the Delegate rights menu.

My application status shows "In Review" â€” how long will it take?

Processing times vary depending on the type of application and the current workload at the registry office. As a general guide: simple renewals and data updates typically take 2â€“5 business days; new registrations may take 5â€“10 business days; closures can take up to 15 business days. You will receive a notification when your application is approved or if additional documents are needed. Avoid submitting a duplicate application while one is already in review.

I accidentally started an application I don't need. Can I delete it?

Yes â€” go to the My Applications tab and find the application. If it shows status Draft (meaning you have not yet submitted it), you will see a Delete button. Click it to remove the draft. Once an application is submitted, it cannot be deleted from the portal â€” you will need to contact the registry office to request a withdrawal.

What is the difference between a Sole Proprietorship and a Company?

A sole proprietorship is owned and operated by one individual â€” you and the business are legally the same person. You are personally liable for all debts. A company (such as a (Pty) Ltd) is a separate legal entity â€” it can own property, sue and be sued, and its debts are generally separate from your personal assets. Companies have more reporting requirements (like annual filing) but offer stronger legal protection. If you are unsure which structure suits your situation, consult a business advisor or the eRegulations portal for guidance.
