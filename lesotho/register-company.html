1. Overview

The Register a Company service on businessregistrations.gov.ls allows you to incorporate a new company in the Kingdom of Lesotho entirely online. Through a single application, you can obtain up to five registrations simultaneously:

RegistrationIssuing AuthorityDescription
Company IncorporationOBFC (Registrar of Companies)Official registration of the company as a legal entity
Business IDOBFCUnique identification number for your business
Business PermitOBFCPermit to operate a business in Lesotho
Trading LicenceMinistry of TradeLicence for commercial trading activities (if applicable)
Industrial LicenceMinistry of TradeLicence for manufacturing/industrial activities (if applicable)
Who can use this service? Any person or entity wishing to form a Private Company, Public Company, Non-Profit Company, or register an External (foreign) Company in Lesotho.

2. Before You Start

What you will need

  • A registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls (you must be logged in to start an application)
  • Your national ID document or passport (scanned copy)
  • Company name — make sure it has not already been taken (you can check during the application)
  • Details of all shareholders, directors, and the company secretary
  • A clear idea of your business activities (sector and specific activity)
  • Banking statement showing proof of local deposit (for business permit)
  • Business plan document (for business permit applications)
Tip: Prepare your documents in advance Scan all required documents as PDF or clear photographs before starting. Accepted formats are PDF, JPG, and PNG. Each file should be under 5 MB.

Company types available

External CompanyForeign company registering a branch or subsidiary in Lesotho

3.1 Welcome Tab — Basic Information

The Welcome tab collects the foundational details about your company and its planned activities.

Type of company

Select the legal form of your company from the dropdown. Choose between Private Company, Public Company, Non-Profit Making Company, or External Company. This selection determines which fields and requirements appear throughout the rest of the form.

Company name

Enter your proposed company name. The system will check whether the name is already registered. If the name already exists, you will be notified and asked to choose a different name.

Company name rules A Private Company name must end with "(Pty) Ltd". A Public Company name must end with "Ltd". Non-profit names must include "NPC" or similar designation.

Business name

If your company will trade under a name different from the registered company name, enter it here. The system also checks whether the business name is already taken. You may use a previously reserved business name if you have one.

Turnover and employees

Enter your projected annual turnover in Maloti (LSL) and the expected number of employees. These figures help determine applicable fees and licensing requirements.

Business activities

Select your primary sector (e.g., Manufacturing, Retail, Services) and specific activity from the dropdown lists. Based on your selections, the system automatically determines whether you need a Trading Licence, Industrial Licence, or both. You can add multiple activities.

The licensing fee is calculated automatically based on your activity selection.

Registered address

Provide the company's registered address, selecting the district and town from the dropdowns. Enter a telephone number and email address for the company.

Operating office

If your operating office is at a different location from the registered address, select "Yes" and provide the operating office details (address, district, town, phone, email).

Tip Click the "Next tab" button at the bottom of each tab to proceed. All required fields must be completed before you can move forward.

3.2 Company Tab — Share Capital

This tab captures information about your company's share structure.

Total amount and shares

Enter the total share capital amount (in Maloti) and the total number of shares to be issued. For example, if your company has a capital of LSL 100,000 with 1,000 shares, each share is worth LSL 100.

Single or multiple shareholders?

Indicate whether the company has a single shareholder or multiple shareholders. This determines the layout for the next step.

3.5 Articles Tab — Articles of Association

The Articles of Association are the rules governing how your company will be run. You have two options:

Option A: Use automated articles (recommended)

Select "Yes" when asked if you will use the automated Articles of Association provided by OBFC. The system will generate a standard set of articles based on the information you have already entered. Click the "Articles of Association" button to generate and preview the document.

Option B: Upload your own articles

Select "No" to upload your own custom Articles of Association. You must then upload a PDF or scanned document. This is typically used by companies with specific governance requirements.

Tip For most standard companies, the automated articles are sufficient and save time. If you have special provisions (e.g., different classes of shares with different voting rights), you may need custom articles.

This tab collects the information required for your business permit application, including applicant personal details, financial information, and staffing.

Applicant personal details

Enter the personal details of the main applicant (the person applying on behalf of the company):

  • Gender, date of birth, nationality
  • National ID number
  • Personal address (district, town)
  • Telephone and email
  • ID Document upload

Staffing

Enter the number of local staff, foreign staff, and the system will calculate the total. If the numbers do not add up, you will be asked to correct them.

Insurance

Business plan and strategy

For the business permit, you must describe:

  • Strategic nature of the business — what makes your business important to Lesotho's economy
  • Contribution to local businesses — how you plan to support local enterprises
  • Capacity transfer plan — how you will train and develop local workforce
  • Corporate social responsibility — your social and environmental commitments

Finally, upload your business plan document.

Important All text areas must be completed thoroughly. Brief or vague answers may result in your application being sent back for revision.

6. What Happens After You Submit

Once you submit your application, it enters a multi-step review and approval process. You can track the status of your application in real time by logging into your account on businessregistrations.gov.ls.

1

Treasury — Payment Verification

The Treasury department verifies that your payment has been received and processed correctly. If there is a payment issue, they will notify you.

2

CI Review — Company Incorporation Review

An officer at OBFC reviews your company incorporation application, checking that all information is correct and documents are in order.

3

CI Approval — Company Incorporation Approval

A senior officer approves or rejects the incorporation. If approved, the system proceeds to generate your company registration. If rejected, you will be notified with reasons.

4

CI Create & Certificate (Automated)

The system automatically creates the company record in the national register and generates your Certificate of Incorporation.

5

BP Review & Approval — Business Permit

Your business permit application is reviewed and approved. This includes assessing your business plan, financial capacity, and staffing details.

6

BP Create & Certificate (Automated)

The business permit is created in the system and your certificate is generated.

7

BR Review & Approval — Business ID

Your Business ID registration is reviewed and approved.

8

BR Create & Certificate (Automated)

Your unique Business ID number is assigned and the certificate is generated.

9

TL / IL Review & Approval — Trading and Industrial Licences

If you applied for trading or industrial licences, these are reviewed and approved by the Ministry of Trade. Each licence follows its own review and approval steps.

10

TL / IL Create & Certificate (Automated)

Once approved, your trading and/or industrial licence certificates are generated automatically. You can download all certificates from your account dashboard.

Sent back for revision? At any review stage, if information is incomplete or incorrect, the officer may send your application back to you for correction. You will receive a notification by email and can see the status in your account. Make the requested corrections and resubmit.

7. Pre-Submission Checklist

Before clicking "Submit", make sure you have completed every item on this list:

  • Selected the correct type of company (Private, Public, Non-Profit, or External)
  • Entered and verified the company name (confirmed it is available)
  • Listed all business activities and confirmed the licence types and fees
  • Provided the registered address with district, town, phone, and email
  • Entered share capital details (total amount and number of shares)
  • Added all shareholders with their ID documents and share allocations
  • Added all directors, the company secretary, and any agents with ID uploads
  • Uploaded or generated the Articles of Association
  • Completed the permit section: applicant details, local deposit, source of funds
  • Uploaded banking statement showing local deposit
  • Entered staffing numbers (local, foreign, and total)
  • Written the strategic nature, local contribution, capacity transfer, and CSR descriptions
  • Uploaded the business plan document
  • Reviewed all fees in the summary and confirmed the total

8. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to register a company?

Processing times vary, but most applications are processed within 5 to 10 working days after payment is confirmed. Complex applications or those requiring corrections may take longer. You can track progress in real time through your online account.

Can I save my application and come back later?

Yes. The system automatically saves your progress. You can log out and return to complete your application at any time. Your draft will be available in your account dashboard under "My Applications".

What if my company name is already taken?

The system checks name availability in real time. If your proposed name is already registered, you will see a message saying the name already exists. You must choose a different name. You can also use a previously reserved business name if you have one linked to your account.

Do I need both a Trading Licence and an Industrial Licence?

It depends on your business activities. If you are only engaged in trading (buying and selling goods), you need a Trading Licence. If you are involved in manufacturing or industrial production, you need an Industrial Licence. If your business does both, you may need both licences. The system determines this automatically based on the activities you select.

Can a foreigner register a company in Lesotho?

Yes. Foreign nationals can register companies in Lesotho. If you are registering a branch of an existing foreign company, select "External Company" as the type. You will need to provide additional information about the parent company, including the country of incorporation, incorporation number, and registration documents.

What happens if my application is sent back for revision?

You will receive an email notification explaining what needs to be corrected. Log into your account, open the application, make the required changes, and resubmit. Common reasons for revision include missing documents, incorrect information, or insufficient detail in the business plan sections.

How do I download my certificates after approval?

Once each registration is approved and the certificate is generated, you will receive a notification. Log into your account on businessregistrations.gov.ls, go to "My Applications", find the completed application, and download each certificate (Certificate of Incorporation, Business ID, Business Permit, and any licence certificates).

Can I use the automated Articles of Association?

Yes, and it is recommended for most companies. The OBFC provides a standard template that covers typical governance requirements. Select "Yes" on the Articles tab and click the button to generate them. If your company has special governance needs (different share classes, specific voting rules, etc.), you should prepare and upload your own articles instead.

What payment methods are accepted?

Fees can be paid digitally through the online payment gateway. The system will direct you to the payment page after you submit your application. Make sure you have a valid payment method ready. After payment is confirmed, your application proceeds to the review stage.

I made an error after submitting. Can I correct it?

Once submitted, you cannot directly edit the application. However, reviewing officers may send the application back to you if they notice errors, giving you an opportunity to make corrections. If you discover an error immediately after submission, contact the OBFC helpdesk for guidance.
