Register a Company

Complete guide to registering a company in Lesotho through the OBFC portal. This single application covers Company Incorporation, Business ID, Business Permit, Trading Licence, and Industrial Licence -- all processed through one unified online form.

1. Overview

The Register a Company service allows you to incorporate a new company in the Kingdom of Lesotho through the One Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC) online portal. By completing a single unified application, you will receive up to five registrations simultaneously:

  • Company Incorporation -- Legal registration of your company under the Companies Act 2011
  • Business ID -- Unique business identification card for your company
  • Business Permit -- Authorization to conduct business operations in Lesotho
  • Trading Licence -- Licence required for commercial trading activities
  • Industrial Licence -- Licence for industrial and manufacturing operations
Key Benefit

Instead of applying separately to each government agency, this service lets you complete all five registrations in one application. The system automatically routes your application to the relevant agencies (Company Registry, Business Permits, Licensing authorities) for parallel processing.

Who Should Use This Service?

This service is for anyone wishing to establish a new company in Lesotho, including:

  • Local entrepreneurs incorporating a private or public company
  • Non-profit organizations seeking incorporation
  • Foreign companies registering an external branch in Lesotho
Before You Begin

You must first reserve a company name through the Name Reservation service before starting this application. The reserved name will be linked to your company registration.

2. Company Types

The form adapts based on the type of company you select. The system supports the following four company types:

Private Company

A company with limited liability where shares are not offered to the general public. This is the most common type of company registration in Lesotho. Private companies have restrictions on transferring shares and may have between 1 and 50 shareholders.

Key characteristics:

  • Share capital is required
  • Can have a sole shareholder or multiple shareholders
  • Must appoint at least one director
  • Articles of association required (automated or own)

Public Company

A company whose shares may be offered to the general public and traded on a stock exchange. Public companies have more stringent reporting and disclosure requirements.

Key characteristics:

  • Share capital is required with higher minimums
  • Must have at least two directors
  • Must appoint a company secretary
  • Subject to additional regulatory requirements

Non-profit Company

A company incorporated not for the financial gain of its members. Non-profit companies are established for charitable, educational, religious, or social purposes. Profits must be used to further the organization's objectives.

Key characteristics:

  • No share capital required
  • Profits cannot be distributed to members
  • Must have a clearly defined non-profit purpose
  • Subject to specific governance requirements

External Company

A company incorporated outside Lesotho that wishes to establish a place of business or branch within the country. External companies must register with the Lesotho authorities and appoint a local representative.

Key characteristics:

  • Must be already incorporated in another jurisdiction
  • Must appoint a local representative in Lesotho
  • Additional documentation from the home jurisdiction is required
  • Subject to both local and home jurisdiction regulations

3. Requirements & Documents

The following documents are required as part of your company registration. Documents vary by registration type and are grouped below.

Company Incorporation Documents

  • Manager's ID -- Certified copy of the identification document of the company manager(s)
  • Shareholder ID -- Certified copy of identification for each shareholder
  • Consent as a Director -- Signed consent form from each person appointed as director

Business ID Documents

  • Lease or Property Title -- Proof of the business premises (lease agreement or property ownership document)
  • Business Owner's National ID -- National identity document of the business owner
  • Applicant Passport -- Valid passport of the applicant (required for foreign nationals)
  • Local Representative ID -- Identification of the appointed local representative (for external companies)
  • Incorporation Document -- Certificate of incorporation or equivalent from the home jurisdiction (for external companies)

Business Permit Documents

  • Banking Statement -- Recent bank statement showing the business account balance
  • Insurance Certificate for Employees -- Proof of employee insurance coverage
  • Business Plan -- A written plan describing the business activities, target market, and financial projections
Trading & Industrial Licences

Trading Licence and Industrial Licence registrations do not require separate document uploads. The information provided in the main application form is sufficient for these registrations.

4. Costs & Fees

Fees are calculated based on the type of company and the licences applied for. The following table shows the standard fee structure. Actual amounts are computed by the system based on your application details.

Registration Fee Type Notes
Company Incorporation Varies by company type Based on share capital and company structure
Business ID Fixed fee Standard fee for business identification card
Business Permit Varies Based on business type and location
Trading Licence Per licence Fee per trading activity listed
Industrial Licence Per licence Fee per industrial activity listed
Payment

Fees are calculated automatically by the system in the Fees section of the application form. Payment is processed digitally through the Treasury step after you submit your application. You will receive a payment reference once your application reaches the Treasury.

5. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to complete your company registration application:

  1. 1

    Create an Account or Log In

    Visit services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and create an account if you do not already have one. If you have an existing account, simply log in with your credentials.

  2. 2

    Reserve a Company Name

    Before starting the company registration, you must reserve a company name through the Name Reservation service. Once your name is approved and reserved, you can proceed with the registration.

  3. 3

    Start the "Register a Company" Application

    From your dashboard, select "Register a Company" to begin a new application. The system will guide you through the form sections.

  4. 4

    Select Company Type

    Choose the type of company you wish to register: Private, Public, Non-profit, or External. The form will adapt to show fields relevant to your selection.

  5. 5

    Complete Company Details

    Fill in the company name, registered address, operating address (if applicable), share capital details, and other required information. Complete all sections including shareholders, executive officers, and activities.

  6. 6

    Add Shareholders & Executive Officers

    Enter details for each shareholder (individual, company, or other corporate entity) and each executive officer (directors, secretary, agent). Each person's identification details and role must be specified.

  7. 7

    Specify Business Activities

    List the business activities your company will undertake. For each activity, indicate whether it requires a Trading Licence, Industrial Licence, or both. The system will calculate the number of licences needed.

  8. 8

    Choose Articles of Association

    Select whether to use the automated Articles of Association provided by OBFC or upload your own Articles of Association. The automated option generates a standard document based on your company details.

  9. 9

    Upload Required Documents

    Upload all required documents listed in Section 3. Accepted file formats include PDF, JPG, and PNG. Ensure documents are clear and legible.

  10. 10

    Review & Submit

    Review all entered information in the summary panel. Verify that all fields are correct and all documents are uploaded. Click "Submit" to send your application for processing.

8. After Submission

Once you submit your application, it progresses through a multi-step review and approval workflow. The following timeline shows the typical processing stages:

Treasury -- Payment Processing

Your application is received by the Treasury for fee assessment and payment processing. You will receive a payment reference.

Step 1 -- Upon submission

Company Incorporation -- Review & Approval

The Company Registry reviews your incorporation documents, verifies company details, and approves the registration. A Certificate of Incorporation is generated automatically.

Steps 2-3 -- Review then Approval

Business Permit -- Review & Approval

The Business Permits office reviews your application and supporting documents to issue the Business Permit.

Steps 4-5 -- Review then Approval

Business ID -- Review & Approval

The Business Registration office processes your Business ID application and issues the business identification card.

Steps 6-7 -- Review then Approval

Trading & Industrial Licences -- Review & Approval

If applicable, your Trading Licence and Industrial Licence applications are reviewed and approved in parallel by the respective licensing authorities.

Steps 8-9 -- Processed in parallel

Certificates Issued

Once all registrations are approved, certificates are generated automatically and made available for download from your dashboard.

Final step -- Certificates available in your account
Track Your Application

You can track the status of your application at any time by logging into your account and viewing your dashboard. Each step is visible with its current status (pending, in review, approved, or returned).

If Your Application Is Returned

If a reviewing officer finds issues with your application, it will be returned to you with comments explaining what needs to be corrected. You can make the requested changes and re-submit without starting a new application.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the entire registration process take?

The typical processing time is 3-7 working days, depending on the completeness of your application and the responsiveness of the reviewing agencies. Applications with all documents properly submitted are processed faster.

Can I save my application and complete it later?

Yes. The system automatically saves your progress. You can log out and return at any time to continue filling in the form from where you left off.

Do I need to visit any government office in person?

No. The entire process is handled online through the OBFC portal. All documents are submitted electronically, and certificates are issued digitally.

What happens if my company name is already taken?

The system will check whether the company name exists in the registry. If the name is already taken, you will need to choose a different name. It is recommended to reserve your company name first through the Name Reservation service.

Can I register a company if I am a foreign national?

Yes. Foreign nationals can register a company in Lesotho. If you are incorporating an External Company (a company already incorporated abroad), you must appoint a local representative in Lesotho and provide additional documentation from your home jurisdiction.

What is the difference between automated and own Articles of Association?

The automated option generates a standard Articles of Association document using information from your application. This is suitable for most companies. If your company has specific governance provisions or shareholder agreements, you can upload your own custom Articles of Association document instead.

How do I know how many Trading or Industrial Licences I need?

The system automatically counts the number of licences based on the activities you list in the Activities section. Each activity that requires a Trading Licence or Industrial Licence is counted separately.

Can I modify my application after submission?

Once submitted, you cannot directly modify your application. However, if a reviewing officer returns your application for corrections, you will be able to make changes and re-submit.

10. Legal Basis

Company registration in Lesotho is governed by the following legislation:

  • Companies Act 2011 -- The primary legislation governing the incorporation, management, and dissolution of companies in Lesotho
  • Trading Enterprises Regulations -- Regulations governing the issuance of trading licences
  • Industrial Licensing Act -- Legislation governing industrial licensing requirements
  • Business Names Registration Act -- Requirements for registering business names

All company registrations processed through the OBFC portal comply with the requirements set out in these laws and their subsidiary regulations.
