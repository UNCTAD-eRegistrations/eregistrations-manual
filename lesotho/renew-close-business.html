How to Renew or Close a Business

Step-by-step citizen guide for the Renew or Close a Business service on businessregistrations.gov.ls

Who is this for? Any person or company registered in Lesotho who needs to renew a Business ID, renew a Trading or Industrial Licence, or formally close (terminate) a business. You must already have an account at businessregistrations.gov.ls.

1 Before You Begin

What You Need

  • A registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls (the same account you used to register your business).
  • Your Business ID number (e.g., BID-2024-00456).
  • Knowledge of which licences you hold — Trading Licence and/or Industrial Licence.
  • A valid mobile money or bank account for online fee payments (M-Pesa, EcoCash, or bank transfer).
✔
Good news: No paper documents need to be uploaded. The system automatically pulls your business data from the Government Data Bridge (GDB), so your Business ID, licence details, and expiry dates are pre-filled.

Understanding the Two Options

This service covers two distinct actions that you select per business:

Action What It Does When to Use
Renew Extends your Business ID and/or licences for a new validity period Your business is expiring within 90 days or has already expired
Close (Terminate) Permanently deregisters your Business ID and/or cancels licences You are ceasing business operations in Lesotho
⚠
Important: Closing a business is permanent. Once your termination is approved, you cannot reverse it. If you wish to operate again, you will need to register a new business from scratch.
2 Log In and Access the Service
  1. Open your browser and go to https://businessregistrations.gov.ls
  2. Click "Log in" in the top right corner.
  3. Enter your email address and password, then click "Sign in".
  4. From the homepage, find and click "Renew or close a business" in the service catalogue.

4 Select Renew or Close for Each Business and Licence

For Business IDs

In the businesses table, for each business you want to act on:

  • Tick the "Renew" checkbox to extend the Business ID validity.
  • Tick the "Close" checkbox to terminate the Business ID permanently.
  • You may only select one option per business — Renew or Close, not both.
  • Leave both unchecked if you do not want to take any action on that business.

For Trading Licences

Below the businesses table, you will see a similar table for Trading Licences. Each row shows the licence number, business it belongs to, type, expiry date, renewal fee, and penalty fee. The same Renew/Close checkboxes apply.

For Industrial Licences

If you hold Industrial Licences, a third table appears. Industrial Licences are required for manufacturing, processing, or industrial activities. Select Renew or Close as needed.

⚠
Closing a Business ID also affects licences: If you close a Business ID, all associated Trading and Industrial Licences under that business will also be terminated. Make sure you review the full impact before proceeding.

💰
Tip for Basotho business owners: Renew early! If you renew your Business ID or licence before it expires, you avoid the penalty fee entirely. The system allows renewal up to 90 days before expiry.
5 Review Your Selections and Submit

After selecting your Renew/Close options, the system generates a summary of all actions and associated fees.

Send Page

Click the "Send" tab to proceed to the submission page. Here you will see:

  • A summary of all Business IDs and Licences you are renewing or closing.
  • The total processing fees and any penalty fees.
  • An email field where your confirmation will be sent.
  • An acknowledgement checkbox you must tick to confirm the accuracy of your information.

6 Payment

After submitting your application, you will be directed to the payment page if any fees are due.

Payment Methods

  • Digital Payment — Pay directly through the platform using mobile money (M-Pesa, EcoCash) or bank transfer. This is the fastest option.
  • In-Person Payment — You may also pay at the OBFC offices in Maseru. Bring your application reference number.
📱
Mobile money tip: Make sure you have sufficient balance in your M-Pesa or EcoCash account before starting the payment. The transaction must complete within the session or you may need to restart the payment step.

Once payment is confirmed, your application moves automatically to the review queue.

8 Types of Registrations Covered

This service handles 7 distinct registration types, grouped by action:

Renewal - Business ID

Extends the validity of your Business ID for another period (typically 1-3 years depending on entity type).

Renewal - Trading Licences

Renews your Trading Licence, required for retail, wholesale, and general commerce operations.

Renewal - Industrial Licences

Renews your Industrial Licence for manufacturing, processing, or factory operations.

Termination - Business ID

Permanently closes your business registration. This deactivates the Business ID in the national registry.

Termination - Trading Licences

Cancels your Trading Licence. Required if you stop trading activities.

Termination - Industrial Licences

Cancels your Industrial Licence. Required if you stop manufacturing or industrial activities.

📋
No documents to upload: Unlike the initial business registration, renewal and closure do not require you to upload any supporting documents. All data is retrieved automatically from the government registry.
9 Frequently Asked Questions
How early can I renew my Business ID before it expires?
You can renew up to 90 days before the expiry date. The system will automatically pre-select businesses expiring within 90 days for renewal. Renewing early avoids any penalty fees and ensures your business remains in good standing.
What happens if my Business ID has already expired?
You can still renew it, but a penalty fee will be added on top of the standard processing fee. The penalty amount depends on how many days past the expiry date you are. Your business may face limitations while expired, so renew as soon as possible.
Can I renew some licences and close others in the same application?
Yes. This is one of the key features of the service. For example, if you own two businesses — say, a shop in Maseru and a workshop in Mafeteng — you can renew one and close the other in the same application. Just select the appropriate checkbox (Renew or Close) for each business and licence.
I have a company (not a sole proprietor). Are there additional steps?
The process is the same for all entity types, including companies, partnerships, and cooperatives. However, companies may have additional licences associated with them. The system will automatically detect if your entity is a company and display all associated licences for action.
How do I know if my renewal was approved?
You will receive an email notification at the address you provided during submission. You can also log into your account at any time and go to "My Applications" to check the status. Approved applications will show a green "Completed" badge, and your updated certificates will be available for download.
What if the officer sends my application back?
If the reviewing officer finds an issue (for example, conflicting selections), your application will be returned to you with a note explaining what needs to be corrected. You will receive an email notification. Log in, make the corrections, and resubmit. You do not need to pay again.
Can I undo a business closure after I submit?
Before approval: Contact the OBFC office in Maseru immediately and ask them to return the application. After approval: Termination is permanent. You would need to register a new business through the "Register a new business" service on the same platform.
Why does my business not appear in the list?
Your business may not appear if: (1) it was registered under a different email address, (2) it is a company and your personal account is not linked as an authorized representative, or (3) the data has not yet been synchronized from the registry. Contact the OBFC help desk at (+266) 2231-7454 for assistance.
I do not have internet access. Can I do this in person?
Yes. You can visit the One Stop Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC) in Maseru, where a staff member will help you complete the process on a computer. Bring your national ID (or passport) and your business registration certificate.
Are the fees the same for sole proprietors and companies?
Processing fees vary by entity type and licence category. The exact fee for your situation is calculated automatically and displayed in the table before you submit. Companies typically have higher renewal fees than sole proprietors.
10 Tips for a Smooth Experience
📅
Set a calendar reminder — Renew at least 60 days before expiry to avoid any last-minute issues. Late renewals attract penalty fees and your business may temporarily lose its active status.
📱
Use a stable internet connection — The application communicates with the Government Data Bridge to pull your business data. A slow or intermittent connection can cause timeouts. WiFi at home, office, or the Maseru public library works well.
💾
Save your application reference number — After submission, note down the reference number displayed on screen and sent to your email. You will need this if you contact the OBFC about your application or for payment at the office.
👥
For companies with multiple directors — Only authorized representatives linked to the company in the registry can manage renewals and closures. If you need to change the authorized representative, this must be done through a separate "Change of Particulars" application first.
💰
Keep payment receipts — Whether you pay by mobile money or bank transfer, keep a record of the transaction. In rare cases, payment confirmation may take up to 24 hours to reflect in the system.
