User Manual

Delegate User Rights

Learn how to delegate access to your business registrations on the OBFC portal, allowing trusted representatives to manage services on your behalf.

1. Overview

The Delegate User Rights service on the OBFC portal allows business owners to grant other users the ability to manage their registrations and services. This is useful when you have accountants, legal representatives, or partners who need to submit filings or manage your business registrations on your behalf.

When you delegate rights, you specify:

  • Who receives the delegated rights (identified by their national ID number)
  • Which business entity the delegation applies to (licence, sole proprietorship, or registered business)
  • Whether the delegate can access all services or only specific ones
  • Whether the delegate is authorised to further share these rights with others
  • The time period during which the delegation is valid
How It Works

Once submitted, the delegation request is processed automatically by the system. The person you delegate rights to must have an account on the OBFC portal. They will gain access to the specified services under your business entity immediately after the request is processed.

Note

This service is free of charge. There are no fees associated with delegating user rights.

2. Types of Delegations

You can delegate access rights for three types of business entities registered on the OBFC portal:

Licence Delegation

Delegate rights for a business that holds a trading licence. You will need to provide the licence number to identify the entity. The delegate will be able to access and manage licence-related services such as renewals and amendments.

Sole Proprietorship / Business Name Delegation

Delegate rights for a sole proprietorship or registered business name. You will need to provide the registration number of the sole proprietorship or business name. The delegate can then manage filings and updates for that entity.

Registered Business (Company) Delegation

Delegate rights for a registered company or business entity. You will need to provide the company registration number. The delegate will gain access to the company's services including annual filings and amendments.

Scope of Access

When delegating rights, you can choose between two levels of access:

  • All Services: The delegate gains access to all current and future services available for your business entity on the portal.
  • Specific Services: You select exactly which services the delegate may access. This is recommended if the delegate only needs to manage specific tasks (e.g., only annual filings).

Right to Sub-Delegate

You can also decide whether the delegate is authorised to further share the rights you grant them with other users. This is controlled by the "Authorised to share rights?" option on the form.

Warning

Be careful when granting the right to sub-delegate. Any person who receives sub-delegated rights will have the same level of access as the person you originally delegated to. Only enable this if you fully trust the delegate to manage access responsibly.

3. Requirements

Before you begin the delegation process, make sure you have the following information ready:

  • Your OBFC account — you must be logged into the OBFC portal
  • National ID number of the person you are delegating to (they must also have an OBFC account)
  • Delegate's full name (first name and family name)
  • Delegate's email address
  • Delegate's nationality
  • Business entity details — the licence number, sole proprietorship number, or company registration number
  • Delegation period — the start ("from") and end ("until") dates for the delegation
No Documents Required

This service does not require you to upload any supporting documents. All information is provided through the online form.

4. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to delegate user rights on the OBFC portal:

  1. 1

    Log In to the OBFC Portal

    Go to services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and log in with your username and password. If you do not have an account, you will need to register first.

  2. 2

    Navigate to "Delegate User Rights"

    From your dashboard, locate and select the "Delegate user rights" service. This will open the delegation form.

  3. 3

    Enter the Delegate's Personal Information

    Fill in the following required fields for the person you wish to delegate rights to:

    • ID Number — the delegate's national identification number
    • First Name and Family Name
    • Nationality — select from the dropdown list
    • Email — the delegate's email address

    Optionally, you may also provide their middle name, gender, date of birth, and phone number.

  4. 4

    Configure Delegation Settings

    Set the delegation parameters:

    • From / Until — specify the start and end dates of the delegation period
    • Authorised to share rights? — choose "Yes" or "No" to control whether the delegate can sub-delegate
    • All or specific services — choose whether to grant access to all services or only selected ones

    If you select "Specific services", an additional dropdown will appear where you can choose which services to include.

  5. 5

    Select the Business Entity

    Choose the type of business entity for which you are delegating rights:

    • Licence — enter the licence number
    • Sole Proprietorship / Business Name — enter the registration number
    • Registered Business (Company) — enter the company registration number
  6. 6

    Review and Submit

    Review all the information you have entered. Ensure the delegate's details and business entity information are correct. When you are satisfied, click the "Submit" button.

  7. 7

    Automatic Processing

    After submission, the system automatically processes your delegation request. You do not need to wait for manual approval. The delegate will gain access to the specified services once processing is complete.

Processing Time

Delegation requests are processed automatically and typically take effect within a few minutes. There is no manual review step.

6. Managing Delegations

After you have delegated user rights, you can manage existing delegations from your dashboard.

Viewing Existing Delegations

All your active delegations are visible in your account dashboard. Each delegation shows:

  • The name and ID of the person you delegated to
  • The business entity involved
  • The scope of access (all services or specific ones)
  • The validity period (from/until dates)
  • Whether sub-delegation is allowed

Modifying a Delegation

To change the scope, duration, or other parameters of an existing delegation, you will need to:

  1. 1

    Revoke the Existing Delegation

    First, revoke the current delegation (see Section 7 below).

  2. 2

    Create a New Delegation

    Submit a new delegation request with the updated parameters.

Tip

When setting delegation dates, consider using an end date ("Until") rather than leaving the delegation open-ended. This ensures access is automatically removed when it is no longer needed.

7. Revoking Access

You may need to revoke delegated rights if the delegate no longer needs access, the delegation period has ended, or you wish to change the scope of access.

Automatic Expiry

If you set an "Until" date when creating the delegation, access will be automatically revoked when that date passes. No further action is required on your part.

Manual Revocation

To manually revoke a delegation before it expires:

  1. 1

    Log In to the OBFC Portal

    Access your account at services.businessregistrations.gov.ls.

  2. 2

    Navigate to Your Delegations

    Go to your dashboard and find the list of active delegations.

  3. 3

    Select and Revoke

    Find the delegation you wish to revoke and follow the portal's instructions to remove it. The delegate will immediately lose access to the services.

Important

If the delegate was authorised to share rights and has already sub-delegated access to others, revoking the delegate's access will also affect all sub-delegated users. Verify the chain of delegation before revoking.
