Overview

The Delegate User Rights service allows business owners, directors, and signatories in Lesotho to grant another person permission to perform actions on their behalf through the eRegistrations portal. This is essential when a business owner wants an accountant, lawyer, manager, or trusted employee to manage registrations, file returns, or handle other company matters online.

Delegation can be set for all services (giving the delegate full access to every available service for the business) or for specific services only (limiting access to particular functions such as filing annual returns or modifying company details). You can also control whether the delegate is authorised to further share rights with other people.

Important: Delegating user rights gives the other person the ability to act on your company's behalf. Only delegate rights to people you trust. You can revoke delegated rights at any time.

Before You Begin

Prerequisites

  • You must have a registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls
  • You must be the owner, director, or an authorised signatory of the business
  • Your business must be in Active status
  • The person you are delegating to must have (or create) their own eRegistrations account

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Log in to the eRegistrations portal Visit businessregistrations.gov.ls and sign in with your credentials.
  2. Select your business From your dashboard, select the business (company, sole proprietorship, or business name/licence) for which you want to delegate rights. Verify the business details shown are correct.
  3. Open the "Delegate User Rights" service Click on Delegate User Rights from the available services menu. The delegation form will open.
  4. Enter the delegate's ID number In the ID number field, enter the national ID or passport number of the person you wish to authorise. The system may auto-populate some details if the person is already registered.
  5. Fill in personal details Complete the delegate's personal information: first name, family name, nationality, email address, and optionally middle name, gender, date of birth, and phone number.
  6. Set the delegation period Specify the From date (when the delegation starts) and the Until date (when it expires). If you want ongoing delegation, you can set a date far in the future.
  7. Choose whether the delegate can share rights Select Yes or No for the question "This person will be authorised to share rights?" If you select Yes, the delegate can further delegate to others.
  8. Select which services to delegate Choose All services to give full access, or Specific services to limit access. If you choose specific services, select the ones you want from the dropdown list.
  9. Submit the delegation Click Delegate user rights and send an email to person being authorised. The system will create the delegation and automatically send an email notification to the delegate.
  10. Confirm and close Once you see the success message, click Register and close application to finalise the process.

Practical Examples

Example 1: Business Owner Delegates to Accountant

Scenario: Malefu owns a textile business in Maseru registered as Malefu Textiles (Pty) Ltd. She wants her accountant, Thabiso Letsie, to file annual returns on her behalf.

Action: Malefu logs in, selects her company, opens Delegate User Rights, enters Thabiso's ID and email, sets "Specific services" and selects "File annual statements". She sets the delegation from today until 31 December 2027 and chooses "No" for sharing rights. Thabiso receives an email and can now file returns for Malefu's company.

Example 2: Director Grants Full Access to Manager

Scenario: Ntate Molapo runs a construction company in Mohale's Hoek. He wants his operations manager, 'Mapalesa, to have full access to all company services on the portal.

Action: Ntate Molapo delegates "All services" to 'Mapalesa with permission to share rights (in case 'Mapalesa needs to involve other team members). The delegation is set without an end date, giving 'Mapalesa ongoing access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I revoke delegated rights later?
Yes. You can modify or remove delegated rights at any time by returning to the Delegate User Rights service and selecting the option to modify existing rights. Look for the "Do you want to modify existing rights?" option in the form.
Does the delegate need their own eRegistrations account?
Yes. The person you delegate to must have (or create) their own account on businessregistrations.gov.ls. They will receive an email invitation with instructions to sign in or register.
Can I delegate rights for a sole proprietorship?
Yes. The delegation service works for all business types registered on the portal, including sole proprietorships, limited liability companies, and business names/licences.
What happens when the delegation period expires?
When the "Until" date passes, the delegate will no longer be able to access services on behalf of your business. You will need to create a new delegation if you wish to extend their access.
Can I delegate rights to someone outside Lesotho?
Yes. You can delegate to anyone regardless of their nationality, as long as they have a valid ID or passport number and an email address. Select the appropriate nationality and phone code when filling in their details.
What does "authorised to share rights" mean?
If you select "Yes" for this option, the person you delegate to will be able to further delegate access to other people on your behalf. For maximum control, select "No" so only you can manage who has access.
Can I delegate to multiple people?
Yes. You can repeat the delegation process for each person you want to authorise. Each delegation is independent -- you can give different people access to different services with different time periods.
I made a mistake in the delegation. Can I correct it?
Yes. Use the "Do you want to modify existing rights?" option in the Delegate User Rights form. You can change the services, time period, or revoke the delegation entirely.
