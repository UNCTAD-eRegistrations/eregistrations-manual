1. Service Overview

This guide walks you through the online registration of a Sole Proprietorship business in Lesotho through the eRegistrations platform.

When you register a sole proprietorship through this service, you receive up to three registrations in a single application:

Registration Issuing Authority Description
Business ID One-Stop Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC) Your official business identity number. All businesses receive this.
Trading Licence Ministry of Trade Required if your activities involve trading (buying and selling goods). Issued based on your selected activities.
Industrial Licence Ministry of Trade Required if your activities involve manufacturing or industrial operations.
Good to Know Not every business needs all three. The system automatically determines which licences you need based on the activities you select. A small shop might only receive a Business ID and Trading Licence, while a garment factory would also receive an Industrial Licence.

Estimated completion time: 15-25 minutes to fill the form online. Processing by government officers typically takes 3-5 working days after submission.

2. Before You Start

Prepare the following before beginning your application.

Create Your Account

Visit businessregistrations.gov.ls and click "Create an account". You will need a valid email address and a mobile phone number. A verification email will be sent to activate your account.

Required Documents

Have digital copies (scanned or photographed) of the following ready to upload:

Document For Details
National ID / Passport All applicants A clear copy of your Lesotho national identity card or passport. For foreigners, a valid passport is required.
Passport-size Photo Business Permit A recent passport-size photograph of the business owner.
Proof of Address Business ID A utility bill, lease agreement, or other document showing your residential address.
Work Permit Foreign nationals only If you are not a Mosotho citizen, you must upload a valid work permit.
File Requirements Upload files in PDF, JPG, or PNG format. Each file should be smaller than 5 MB. Make sure documents are clearly legible - blurry or cut-off images may cause delays.

Information You Will Need

  • Your full legal name (as it appears on your ID)
  • Date of birth, nationality, and gender
  • Residential address (district, village/town, physical address)
  • Contact email and phone number
  • Your proposed business name (must be unique)
  • The business activities you plan to carry out
  • Location of your business premises
  • Estimated annual turnover and number of employees

How Business Name Checking Works

When you enter a business name, the system performs an automatic check against the national business registry. There are two possible outcomes:

ResultWhat It MeansWhat To Do
Name Available No identical name exists in the registry Proceed with the application. Your name will be reserved upon submission.
Name Taken An identical or very similar name is already registered You must choose a different name. Try adding a distinguishing word, e.g., "Palesa Trading Maseru" instead of "Palesa Trading".
Tips for Choosing a Business Name
  • Use a distinctive name that reflects your business -- e.g., Lerato's Catering Services, Thabo Auto Repairs
  • Avoid generic names like "Maseru Trading" as they are likely already taken
  • You can include your own name to make it unique, e.g., Mokoena Construction
  • The name must not be offensive or misleading
  • You can optionally add a separate business name for each activity (more on this in the Activities section)

Annual Turnover

Enter your estimated annual revenue in Lesotho Maloti (LSL). This figure is used to determine your licence fees:

  • M 5,000,000 or less: Standard fee tier for small businesses
  • More than M 5,000,000: Higher fee tier for larger businesses

Be honest in your estimate. Providing a false turnover figure may lead to penalties later.

How to Select Activities

  1. Click "Add activity" A dialog will open where you can search for and select your business activity from a standardised list (ISIC classification).
  2. Search for your activity Type keywords to find your activity. For example, type "restaurant" to find food service activities, or "construction" for building activities.
  3. Select the most appropriate match Choose the activity that best describes what your business will do. The system will automatically assign the correct licence type (Trading or Industrial).
  4. Optionally add a business name for the activity If you want this specific activity to operate under a different name, you can enter one. Otherwise, it uses your main business name.
  5. Add more activities if needed You can repeat the process for multiple activities. For example, a shop owner might add "Retail trade" and "Wholesale trade" as separate activities.
Trading vs. Industrial Licence

Activities are automatically categorised:

  • Trading Licence covers buying and selling goods, retail, wholesale, hospitality, and services
  • Industrial Licence covers manufacturing, processing, and industrial production activities

Some businesses need both -- for example, a bakery that makes bread (industrial) and sells it in a shop (trading).

Restrictions for Foreign Nationals Certain business activities are restricted for non-Basotho nationals. If you selected "Other country" on the guide page, some activities may not be available to you. The system will indicate which activities are restricted.

Fees are calculated automatically based on your selected activities and annual turnover.

Fee Structure

The fees for a sole proprietorship depend on the type of licences you are applying for and your estimated annual turnover. Below are the typical fee ranges:

Varies by activity

Pro-Rated Fees If you register your business after 30 June (second half of the financial year), your licence fee may be pro-rated. The system handles this calculation automatically based on the date of your application.

Payment Methods

MethodHow It WorksProcessing Time
Online Payment (iVeri) Pay by debit or credit card through the secure iVeri payment gateway. You will be redirected to the payment page after submitting your application. Instant confirmation

Payment Timing Your application will not be fully processed until payment is confirmed. If you choose to pay at the cashier, your application will wait in the queue until the cashier confirms receipt of payment. Paying online is faster and recommended.

11. What Happens After Submission

Once you submit, your application goes through several review stages. Here is what happens behind the scenes.

A

Activity Name Reservation Automatic

The system automatically reserves your proposed business and activity names so no one else can register the same names while your application is being processed.

1

Business Permit Review Officer Review

An OBFC officer reviews your business permit application, checks your personal details, documents, and proposed activities. If something is missing or incorrect, they may send the application back to you for corrections.

2

Business Permit Validation Senior Officer

A senior officer validates the reviewed application. This is a quality assurance step to ensure all information is accurate before proceeding.

B

Business Permit & Person Records Created Automatic

The system automatically creates your business permit record and your person record in the national registry.

3

Business ID Review Officer Review

An officer reviews your Business ID application, verifying your identity documents and business information.

4

Business ID Validation Senior Officer

A senior officer validates and approves your Business ID. Once approved, your unique Business ID number is generated.

C

Business ID Created & Certificate Issued Automatic

Your Business ID is automatically registered in the system and your Business ID certificate is generated.

5

Trading Licence Review & Validation Officer Review

If your activities require a trading licence, an officer reviews and validates it. This includes checking that your activities match the correct licence category and fee.

6

Industrial Licence Review & Validation Officer Review

If your activities require an industrial licence, a separate officer reviews and validates it. This step only applies if you have industrial activities.

✓

Certificates Issued Automatic

All approved certificates (Business ID, Trading Licence, Industrial Licence) are generated and become available for download in your account. You will receive a notification email.

Application Sent Back? If an officer finds issues with your application (missing documents, unclear information, errors), they will send it back to you with comments explaining what needs to be corrected. You will receive an email notification. Log into your account, review the comments, make corrections, and resubmit.

Tracking Your Application

You can track the status of your application at any time by logging into your account at businessregistrations.gov.ls. The dashboard shows:

  • Current status of each registration (pending, under review, approved, sent back)
  • Which step your application is currently at
  • Any comments from reviewing officers
  • Download links for issued certificates

12. Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions from applicants.

How long does the entire registration process take? +

Filling out the online form typically takes 15-25 minutes if you have all your documents ready. After submission, the government review and approval process usually takes 3-5 working days, depending on the workload at the OBFC. If your application is sent back for corrections, this timeline resets from the date you resubmit.

Can I register a business if I am not a Lesotho citizen? +

Yes, foreign nationals can register a sole proprietorship in Lesotho. However, you will need a valid work permit and passport. Certain business activities are restricted for non-Basotho citizens under the Trading Enterprise Regulations. The system will indicate which activities are not available to you.

What if my proposed business name is already taken? +

The system will notify you during the application that the name is unavailable. You will need to choose a different name. Try adding a distinguishing word -- for example, instead of "Maseru Catering", try "Maseru Home Catering" or "Maseru Catering by Lerato". You can also use your own name as part of the business name, such as Mokoena's General Dealer.

Can I save my application and complete it later? +

Yes. Click "Save Draft" at any point during the application. Your progress will be saved to your account. When you log in again, go to your dashboard and find the draft application to continue. Drafts do not expire, but it is recommended to complete your application within a few weeks.

Do I need to visit the OBFC office in person? +

Not necessarily. If you sign electronically and pay online (via iVeri), the entire process can be completed online. You only need to visit the office if you choose to sign manually or pay at the cashier counter. Your certificates will be available for download from your online account once approved.

What is the difference between a Trading Licence and an Industrial Licence? +

A Trading Licence is required for activities involving the buying and selling of goods and services -- retail shops, restaurants, wholesalers, transport companies, etc. An Industrial Licence is required for manufacturing and processing activities -- factories, workshops, food processing plants, etc. Some businesses need both. The system automatically determines which licences you need based on the activities you select.

My application was sent back. What do I do? +

Do not panic -- this is common and usually easy to fix. Log into your account and open the application. You will see comments from the reviewing officer explaining what needs to be corrected. Common reasons include: unclear document uploads, missing information, or a business name conflict. Make the requested corrections and click "Submit" again. Your application will return to the officer for re-review.

Can I add more business activities after registration? +

Yes. After your business is registered, you can apply for amendments through the eRegistrations portal. Log into your account, find your registered business, and select the option to add or modify activities. This may require additional fees and a new review process.

How much does it cost to register a sole proprietorship? +

The Business ID registration itself is free. Licence fees depend on your activities and turnover. Trading licences typically range from M 500 to M 3,000 for standard activities, with special categories costing up to M 15,000. The exact fees are calculated and displayed before you submit your application, so there are no surprises. Fees may be pro-rated if you register in the second half of the financial year (after 30 June).

I registered for someone else. How do they get access to the business account? +

If you registered the business on behalf of another person, the owner can request user rights through the system. During the submission step, you are asked whether the applicant will receive user rights. If "Yes" was selected, the business owner will be able to manage the business through their own eRegistrations account once the registration is complete.
