Register a Business

Complete guide to registering your business in Lesotho through the One Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC). This single application covers your Business ID, Business Permit, Trading Licence, Industrial Licence, and Company Incorporation.

1. Overview

The Register a Business service allows you to register a new business in the Kingdom of Lesotho through a single online application. The One Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC) coordinates multiple government agencies so that you complete just one form and receive all the registrations, permits, and licences you need to legally operate your business.

Through this joint registration process, your single application is processed simultaneously by:

  • Registrar of Companies — issues your Business ID and handles Company Incorporation
  • Ministry of Trade and Industry — issues your Trading Licence and Industrial Licence
  • Ministry of Small Business — issues your Business Permit
Key Information

The entire process is conducted online. You do not need to visit any government office in person. Most applications are processed within 2–3 business days.

How It Works

The application process is divided into two parts:

  • Part A — Guide: You answer 3 quick questions about your business type, who is applying, and the owner's nationality. The system uses your answers to customise the form and show only the fields and documents relevant to your situation.
  • Part B — Application: You fill in your personal details, business information, upload required documents, and submit the application for processing.

2. What You Will Receive

Upon successful processing of your application, you will receive the following documents and registrations:

Document / Registration Issuing Agency Description
Business ID Certificate Registrar of Companies Your unique business identification number, required before any other registrations can be issued
Business Permit Ministry of Small Business Authorisation to conduct business activities in Lesotho
Trading Licence Ministry of Trade & Industry Licence to engage in trading activities
Industrial Licence Ministry of Trade & Industry Licence for manufacturing and industrial operations
Certificate of Incorporation Registrar of Companies Formal registration of a company as a legal entity (companies only)
Articles of Association Registrar of Companies The company's governing document (companies only)
Tax Identification Number (TIN) Lesotho Revenue Authority Automatically assigned through integration with LRA systems
Note

Not all documents apply to every applicant. For example, Company Incorporation and Articles of Association are only issued to companies, not sole proprietors. The system automatically determines which registrations apply based on your answers in the Guide.

3. Who Can Apply

The service is available to the following types of business entities:

Sole Proprietor

A business owned and operated by a single individual. This is the simplest form of business registration. The owner and the business are legally the same entity.

Who can register: Any individual aged 18 or older, whether a Lesotho national or a foreign national with the right to operate a business in Lesotho.

Registrations issued: Business ID, Business Permit, Trading Licence, and/or Industrial Licence.

Private Company

A company with limited liability where shares are privately held and not available to the general public. Requires at least one director and one shareholder.

Who can register: Any person or group of persons wishing to form a private limited company under the Companies Act 2011.

Registrations issued: Business ID, Certificate of Incorporation, Articles of Association, Business Permit, Trading Licence, and/or Industrial Licence.

Public Company

A company whose shares may be offered to and traded by the general public. Subject to additional regulatory requirements.

Who can register: Persons forming a company that intends to offer shares to the public, meeting the minimum capitalisation requirements.

Registrations issued: Business ID, Certificate of Incorporation, Articles of Association, Business Permit, Trading Licence, and/or Industrial Licence.

External Company

A company incorporated outside Lesotho that wishes to establish a place of business within Lesotho.

Who can register: Any foreign company seeking to operate in Lesotho. Must appoint a local representative.

Registrations issued: Business ID, Business Permit, Trading Licence, and/or Industrial Licence.

Not-for-Profit Company

A company formed for purposes other than generating profit for its members. Income is used to further the company's objectives.

Who can register: Any group of persons wishing to form a not-for-profit entity under the Companies Act 2011.

Registrations issued: Business ID, Certificate of Incorporation, Articles of Association, Business Permit.

Applying on Behalf of Someone Else

You may submit an application on behalf of another person (the business owner). When you select this option in the Guide, the form will ask for both the applicant's details and the business owner's details separately.

4. Requirements

Before starting your application, gather the following documents and information. The exact requirements depend on your business type.

Common Requirements (All Applicants)

  • National ID or Passport — A valid identity document for the business owner. Lesotho nationals must provide their national ID; foreign nationals must provide a passport copy.
  • Lease Agreement or Property Title — Proof of your business premises (certificate of occupancy, lease agreement, or property title).
  • Email Address — A valid email address for account creation and notifications.
  • Phone Number — A contact telephone number.
  • Business Plan — A description of your planned business activities (required for the Business Permit).

Additional Requirements by Business Type

Sole Proprietor

No additional documents are typically required beyond the common requirements listed above. If you are a foreign national, see the "Foreign Owner" tab.

Company Registration

In addition to the common requirements, companies must provide:

  • Directors' and Managers' ID Documents — National ID or passport for each director and manager.
  • Shareholders' ID Documents — National ID or passport for each shareholder.
  • Consent to Act as Director — A signed consent form for each proposed director.
  • Banking Statement — Recent bank statement to demonstrate financial standing.
  • Insurance Certificate — Insurance certificate for employees (if applicable).
  • Shareholder and Director Details — Full names, addresses, nationality, and share allocation for each shareholder and director.

Foreign Business Owner

If the business owner is not a Lesotho national, the following additional requirements apply:

  • Passport Copy — Valid passport of the foreign business owner.
  • Local Representative's ID — National ID of a designated local representative in Lesotho.
  • Incorporation Documents — If registering a foreign (external) company, certified copies of the company's incorporation documents from the country of origin.
Important

All uploaded documents must be clear, legible scans or photos. Accepted file formats are PDF, JPG, and PNG. Each file must be under 5MB in size. Incomplete or illegible documents will result in your application being returned for corrections.

5. Costs & Fees

The fees for registering a business in Lesotho depend on your business type, size, and the registrations you require. All fees are payable online through the platform.

Note

Company Incorporation and Business Permit registration are currently free of charge. The system will calculate your total fees automatically based on the information you provide in the application. Payment is made online once your application has been validated by officials.

6. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to complete your business registration application.

Step 1: Create an Account

  1. 1

    Visit the OBFC Portal

    Go to services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and click "Register" to create a new account.

  2. 2

    Verify Your Email

    Enter your email address and create a password. You will receive a verification email. Click the link in the email to activate your account.

  3. 3

    Log In

    Return to the portal and log in with your email and password. You will be taken to your dashboard.

Step 2: Start the Application

  1. 4

    Select "Register a Business"

    From your dashboard, find and click the "Register a Business" service to start a new application.

Step 3: Complete the Guide (Part A)

The Guide asks three questions that determine which sections of the form will appear:

  1. 5

    Question 1: Business Type

    Select whether you are registering as a Sole Proprietor or a Company. If you choose "Company", additional sections for shareholders, directors, and company details will appear.

  2. 6

    Question 2: Who Is Applying?

    Select whether you are the business owner or you are creating this business for another person. If applying for someone else, you will need to provide both your details and the owner's details.

  3. 7

    Question 3: Owner's Nationality

    Select whether the business owner is from Lesotho or from another country. Foreign owners will need to provide additional documentation, including a local representative.

Step 4: Fill in Personal Details

  1. 8

    Enter Your Personal Information

    Complete the following fields:

    Field Type Required
    First NameTextYes
    Middle NamesTextNo
    SurnameTextYes
    GenderDropdownYes
    Date of BirthDateYes
    NationalityDropdownYes
    ID NumberTextYes
    Marital StatusDropdownYes
    TelephoneTextYes
    EmailTextYes

Step 5: Enter Address Details

  1. 9

    Provide Your Address

    Indicate whether your residential address is in Lesotho. Then fill in the address fields: village/town, street name or chief's name, district, and country.

Step 6: Provide Business Details

  1. 10

    Business Information

    Enter the details of your business including:

    • Proposed business name — The name under which you will trade
    • Business activities — Add one or more activities from the classification list
    • Business address — Physical location of your business premises
    • Number of employees — Expected number of workers
    • Expected annual turnover — Estimated yearly revenue (determines fee category)
    • Branch offices — If your business has additional operating locations, add them here

Step 7: Company Details (Companies Only)

  1. 11

    Shareholders and Directors

    If you are registering a company, you must also provide:

    • Type of company — Private, Public, External, or Not-for-Profit
    • Shareholders — Full name, nationality, address, ID document, and number of shares for each shareholder
    • Directors and Managers — Full name, nationality, address, ID document, and consent form for each director
    • Share types — Whether the company has only ordinary shares or also preferred shares
    • Articles of Association — Choose to generate them automatically or upload a custom version
    • Signature preference — Electronic or manual signature for the Memorandum of Association

Step 8: Upload Documents

  1. 12

    Upload Required Documents

    Upload all required supporting documents. The form will indicate which documents are mandatory based on your business type. Use the upload buttons next to each document field.

    Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG (maximum 5MB per file).

Step 9: Review and Submit

  1. 13

    Review Your Application

    Carefully review all the information you have entered. Use the navigation menu on the left side of the form to jump between sections. Once satisfied, click the "Submit" button.

Step 10: Make Payment

  1. 14

    Pay the Registration Fees

    After your application has been validated by officials, you will receive a notification to make payment. Log in to your account, go to the payment section, and complete the online payment. Your registrations will be finalised once payment is confirmed.

Important

You can save your application at any time and return to complete it later. Your progress is automatically saved. However, the application will not be processed until you click "Submit".

7. After Submission

Once you submit your application, it goes through the following processing stages:

Application Submitted

Your application is received and assigned a unique reference number. You will receive an email confirmation.

Day 0

Activity Names Reserved

The system automatically verifies and reserves your chosen business activity names.

Day 0 (automated)

Business ID Review

The Registrar of Companies reviews your Business ID application and verifies your identity documents.

Day 1

Business Permit Validation

The Ministry of Small Business reviews your business permit application and supporting documents.

Day 1–2

Licence Reviews

The Ministry of Trade and Industry reviews your Trading Licence and/or Industrial Licence applications.

Day 1–2

Company Incorporation Review

If registering a company, the Registrar reviews your incorporation documents, articles of association, and shareholder details.

Day 1–2 (companies only)

Payment

Once all reviews are complete, you receive a payment notification. Complete the online payment to proceed.

Day 2–3

Certificates Issued

After payment confirmation, all your certificates, permits, and licences are generated and made available for download from your account.

Day 2–3
Corrections

If any information in your application needs to be corrected, officials will send it back to you with instructions on what to fix. You will receive an email notification. Log in, make the requested changes, and re-submit.

8. Tracking Your Application

You can monitor the status of your application at any time through your account.

  1. 1

    Log In to Your Account

    Visit services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and sign in with your credentials.

  2. 2

    Go to "My Applications"

    Click on "My Applications" to see all your submitted applications.

  3. 3

    Check the Status

    Each registration within your application shows its current status: Submitted, Under Review, Awaiting Payment, Approved, or Returned for Correction.

  4. 4

    Download Your Documents

    Once your registrations are approved and payment is confirmed, you can download your certificates, permits, and licences directly from your account.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the registration take?

Most applications are processed within 2–3 business days, provided all documents are complete and correct. Applications that need corrections may take longer.

Can I save my application and come back later?

Yes. Your progress is automatically saved. You can log out and return at any time to continue your application before submitting it.

What happens if my application is returned?

If officials find issues with your application, it will be sent back to you with specific instructions on what needs to be corrected. You will receive an email notification. Log in, make the changes, and re-submit.

Do I need to visit a government office?

No. The entire process is conducted online through the OBFC portal. You do not need to visit any office in person.

Can I apply on behalf of someone else?

Yes. When completing the Guide (Part A), select the option indicating you are creating the business for another person. You will need to provide both your details and the business owner's details.

What is an MSME?

MSME stands for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprise. A business qualifies as an MSME if its annual turnover is LSL 5,000,000 or less and it employs 50 or fewer people. MSMEs pay reduced registration fees.

Do I need both a Trading Licence and an Industrial Licence?

Not necessarily. A Trading Licence is for businesses engaged in buying and selling goods. An Industrial Licence is for businesses engaged in manufacturing or industrial activities. Some businesses may need both, depending on their activities.

Can foreign nationals register a business in Lesotho?

Yes. Foreign nationals can register a business but must provide a passport copy and designate a local representative in Lesotho. External (foreign) companies must also provide their incorporation documents from the country of origin.

What payment methods are accepted?

Payment is made online through the platform. Accepted methods include mobile money and bank transfer. The exact amount will be calculated based on your business type and size.

How do I get help with my application?

You can contact the OBFC Help Desk by phone, email, or by visiting the OBFC office in Maseru. See the Contact & Support section below for details.

10. Legal Basis

The business registration process is governed by the following legislation:

Legislation Purpose
Companies Act, 2011 Governs the formation, management, and dissolution of companies in Lesotho
Trading Enterprises Act, 1993 Regulates trading licences and business activities
Industrial Licensing Act, 1969 Governs industrial licences and manufacturing operations
Business Names Registration Act Regulates the registration and use of business names
One Business Facilitation Centre Order, 2020 Establishes the OBFC as a single window for business registration
