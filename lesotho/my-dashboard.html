1. Overview

The My Dashboard is your personalised control centre on the Lesotho eRegistrations platform. After logging in, the dashboard gives you a single place to manage every aspect of your business life in Lesotho — from registering a new sole proprietorship or company, to renewing licences, tracking the status of pending applications, and accessing useful government services.

Key capabilities of the dashboard include:

  • Viewing all your registered sole proprietorships and companies at a glance
  • Tracking the status and renewal dates of each business and its linked activities/licences
  • Performing actions such as renewing, closing, updating data, and delegating rights directly from the dashboard
  • Monitoring all your submitted applications and their processing status
  • Accessing public business registries (sole proprietorships, companies, beneficial ownership)
  • Navigating to useful external links such as tax clearance, trade portal, and eCustoms
Tip

The dashboard is free to use and available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. You can access it from any device with a web browser and an internet connection.

2. Accessing Your Dashboard

Follow these steps to access your personal dashboard:

  1. 1

    Open the Platform

    Navigate to services.businessregistrations.gov.ls using any modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge).

  2. 2

    Log In to Your Account

    Click the "Login | Register" button in the top-right corner of the page. Enter your registered email address and password. If you do not yet have an account, click "Register" to create one.

  3. 3

    Arrive at Your Dashboard

    After a successful login, you will be taken to the Welcome tab. From here, you can verify your identity or navigate to any of the dashboard tabs to manage your businesses and applications.

Important

Keep your login credentials secure and do not share them with others. If you suspect unauthorised access to your account, change your password immediately and contact OBFC support.

3. Dashboard Layout & Navigation

Your dashboard is organised into several tabs, each providing access to different features. The main navigation tabs appear at the top of the dashboard area:

Welcome

The first screen you see after logging in. It displays a welcome message and provides a button to verify your identity (KYC) if you have not done so already. Identity verification is required before you can register a business.

My Dashboard

Your main overview page. Shows all your registered sole proprietorships and companies, each with their Business ID, name, type, status, renewal date, and linked activities/licences. You can take actions (renew, close, update, delegate rights) directly from here.

My Applications

A paginated list of all applications you have submitted. Each entry shows the service name, company name, creation date, submission date, and current processing status. You can also delete draft applications that have not yet been submitted.

Business ID

Detailed information about each of your businesses, identified by their unique Business ID number.

Activities, Licences & Business Names

A consolidated view of all the business activities and licences linked to your businesses, along with their renewal dates and statuses.

Useful Links

Quick-access buttons to external government services and portals including tax clearance, trade portal, tourism licensing, eCustoms, and more.

Business Registries

Access the public registries to search for sole proprietorships, companies, and beneficial ownership information.

4. Identity Verification (KYC)

Before you can register a business or perform certain actions on the platform, you must verify your identity. This process is known as Know Your Customer (KYC) verification.

  1. 1

    Navigate to the Welcome Tab

    After logging in, you will land on the Welcome tab by default. If you are on another tab, click "Welcome" in the navigation bar.

  2. 2

    Click "Verify Your Identity"

    Click the "Verify your identity" button. This will launch the identity verification process powered by the platform's KYC provider.

  3. 3

    Complete the Verification

    Follow the on-screen instructions to upload your identification documents and complete the verification. You may be asked to provide a government-issued ID and a selfie.

  4. 4

    Check Your KYC Status

    Once submitted, your KYC status will be updated on the dashboard. You can check the current status at any time. Verification is typically processed quickly, but may take up to 24 hours in some cases.

Note

Identity verification is a one-time process. Once verified, you will not need to repeat it unless your identity documents expire or there is a change in your personal information.

5. Managing Your Businesses

The My Dashboard tab provides a comprehensive overview of all your registered businesses. Businesses are organised into two categories: Sole Proprietorships and Companies.

Sole Proprietorships

Each sole proprietorship entry displays the following information:

Field Description
Business ID The unique identifier assigned to your business upon registration
Name The registered trading name of your sole proprietorship
Type The type of business entity (sole proprietorship)
Status Current registration status (active, expired, etc.)
Renewal Date The date by which the business registration must be renewed
No. of Activities The number of licensed business activities linked to this business
Linked Activities A detailed list of each activity/licence, including licence ID, business name, sector, and renewal date

Companies

Company entries display similar information, with additional fields:

Field Description
Business ID Unique identifier for the company
Name The registered company name
Legal Person Number The legal entity registration number
Type of Business The company type classification
Renewal Date The date by which the company registration must be renewed
No. of Activities Number of licensed activities linked to the company
Linked Activities Detailed listing of all linked licences and activities
Tip

If you do not have any businesses registered yet, the dashboard will display a message with a button to register a new sole proprietorship or create a new company. Click the appropriate button to start the registration process.

6. Viewing & Tracking Applications

The My Applications tab shows a complete list of all applications you have created or submitted through the platform. This includes registrations, renewals, amendments, and closures.

Application Information

Each application entry displays:

Column Description
Service The type of service or registration the application relates to
Company The business name associated with the application
Created The date and time the application was first created (as a draft)
Submitted The date and time the application was officially submitted for processing
Status The current processing status of the application

Application Status Stages

Draft

Application has been created but not yet submitted. You can still edit or delete it.

Submitted

Application has been submitted and is awaiting review by the relevant authority.

Under Review

Your application is being reviewed by government officials.

Approved

Your application has been approved and the registration or licence is active.

Returned / Requires Action

Additional information or corrections are needed. Check the application for details and resubmit.

Deleting a Draft Application

If you have created an application but have not yet submitted it, you can delete it:

  1. 1

    Locate the Draft

    Go to the My Applications tab and find the application with "Draft" status.

  2. 2

    Click the Delete Option

    Click the delete action on the draft application. A confirmation message will appear.

  3. 3

    Confirm Deletion

    Click "Delete file" to confirm, or "Cancel" to keep the application.

Warning

Deleting a draft application is permanent and cannot be undone. Only applications that have not yet been submitted can be deleted. Once submitted, applications cannot be removed by the applicant.

Pagination

If you have many applications, the list is paginated. Use the Previous and Next buttons at the bottom of the list to navigate between pages. You can also adjust the number of rows per page using the dropdown selector.

7. Business Actions & Services

From the My Dashboard tab, you can perform a number of actions on each of your registered businesses. The available actions depend on the type of business and its current status.

Sole Proprietorship Actions

For each sole proprietorship, you can access the following actions through the action menu:

  • Update Data — Modify the registered details of your sole proprietorship, such as business address or contact information
  • Renew Business — Renew your business registration before or after it expires to maintain active status
  • Delegate Rights — Authorise another person to manage or take actions on your business on your behalf
  • Close Business — Formally close your sole proprietorship and deactivate the registration

Company Actions

For registered companies, the following actions are available:

  • Update Data — Update company details including directors, shareholders, registered address, and contact information
  • Annual Filing — Submit required annual returns and filings for your company
  • Delegate Rights — Authorise another person to act on behalf of the company
  • Close Business — Initiate the formal closure or winding up of the company

Activity/Licence Actions

Within each business, you can also perform actions on individual licences and activities:

  • Renew or Close — Renew a specific business activity licence, or close it if no longer needed
  • Change Business Name — Request a name change for a specific business activity
  • Delegate Right to a Licence — Grant another person rights to manage a specific licence
Note

Some actions may only be available to users who have been granted authorisation rights. If you are a delegated user, you may see a limited set of actions depending on the permissions granted to you by the business owner.

8. Business Registries

The Business Registries tab provides access to Lesotho's public business registers. These are searchable databases where you can look up information about any registered business in the country.

Three registries are available:

Sole Proprietorships Registry

Search the register of all sole proprietorships registered in Lesotho. You can look up businesses by name, Business ID, or owner name to verify registration status and details.

Companies Registry

Search the register of all companies incorporated in Lesotho. View company details including legal person number, directors, and registration status.

Beneficial Ownership Registry

Access the beneficial ownership register to identify the natural persons who ultimately own or control a registered company. This registry supports transparency and anti-money laundering requirements.

Tip

The business registries are publicly accessible. You do not need to be logged in to search them, but being logged in allows you to navigate directly from the dashboard.

10. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I register a new business?

From the My Dashboard tab, if you have no businesses yet, click the "Register a new sole proprietorship" or "New company" button. If you already have businesses, you can start a new registration from the relevant services page. You must complete identity verification (KYC) before registering.

What does it mean when my business status shows "Expired"?

An "Expired" status indicates that your business registration has passed its renewal date without being renewed. You should renew it as soon as possible by clicking "Renew Business" from the action menu on your dashboard. Late renewals may incur additional fees.

Can I manage businesses on behalf of someone else?

Yes. A business owner can delegate rights to another registered user. Once rights are delegated, the authorised person will see the business on their own dashboard and can perform the permitted actions.

How do I check if my application has been processed?

Go to the My Applications tab. The Status column shows the current state of each application. If the status is "Approved," your application has been successfully processed. If it shows "Returned," additional action is required from you.

Can I delete a submitted application?

No. Only draft applications (those not yet submitted) can be deleted. Once an application is submitted, it enters the processing workflow and can no longer be removed by the applicant. If you submitted an application in error, contact OBFC support for assistance.

What is a Business ID?

A Business ID is a unique identification number assigned to your business upon registration. It is used across all government systems to identify your business. Keep this number safe as you will need it for renewals, licence applications, and other government interactions.

What is the difference between a Business ID and a Licence ID?

A Business ID identifies your business entity (sole proprietorship or company). A Licence ID identifies a specific business activity or licence linked to that business. One business can have multiple licences for different activities.

How long does identity verification take?

Identity verification is usually completed within minutes. In some cases, manual review may be required, which can take up to 24 hours. You will be notified of the outcome on your dashboard.
