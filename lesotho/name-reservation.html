User Manual

Name Reservation

Reserve a unique business name before registering your company in Lesotho. This online service allows you to check name availability and secure your preferred business name through the One Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC).

1. Overview

The Name Reservation service allows you to reserve a unique business name before formally registering your company with the Registrar of Companies in Lesotho. A reserved name is held exclusively for you, ensuring no other business can register under the same name during the reservation period.

This service is available online through the OBFC eRegistrations platform. You can propose up to three alternative names and the system will check each one for availability in real time. Once you submit your application, a government officer will review and approve your preferred name.

Who is this for?

This service is for anyone intending to register a business in Lesotho, including sole proprietors, partnerships, private companies, and public companies. Name reservation is a recommended first step before company registration.

What you will need

  • An OBFC online account (free to create)
  • Up to three proposed business names
  • Details about the type of business you intend to register
  • Personal information of the business owner (name, date of birth, nationality)
  • A copy of your National ID document
  • Contact details and address

How long does it take?

The online application takes approximately 10 to 15 minutes to complete. Processing by the Registrar typically takes 1 to 3 business days after submission.

2. Requirements

Before starting your application, make sure you have the following items ready:

Documents

  • National ID — A scanned copy or clear photograph of your national identity document (passport or national ID card). Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, or PNG.

Information

  • Proposed business names — Up to 3 name options in order of preference
  • Type of business — Whether the entity is a sole proprietor, partnership, or company
  • Type of company — If registering a company, whether it is private or public
  • Owner details — Full name, surname, gender, date of birth, and nationality
  • Contact details — Physical address, municipality, phone number, and email address
Important

The names you propose must be unique and not already registered by another business in Lesotho. The system will check each name in real time, but final approval is subject to review by the Registrar of Companies.

4. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to complete your name reservation application online.

  1. 1

    Create an Account or Log In

    Visit services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and either create a new account or log into your existing one. You will need a valid email address to register.

  2. 2

    Select Name Reservation Service

    From the service catalogue, select Name Reservation. You will see an introductory page explaining the service and its requirements.

  3. 3

    Choose Your Business Type

    On the "Ready to start?" tab, select the type of business you want to register (e.g., Sole Proprietor, Partnership, Company). If you selected "Company", also specify the type of company (Private or Public) and the company classification.

  4. 4

    Click "Start!" to Begin

    After reviewing the information and selecting your business type, click the Start! button to proceed to the application form.

  5. 5

    Propose Your Business Names

    On the "Name" tab, enter up to three proposed business names:

    • Proposed name #1 — Your first-choice name (required)
    • Proposed name #2 — Your second-choice name (optional)
    • Proposed name #3 — Your third-choice name (optional)

    After entering each name, click the corresponding Check button (Check #1, Check #2, or Check #3) to verify whether the name is already taken. The system will display whether the name exists or is available.

  6. 6

    Click "Next Tab" to Continue

    Once you have entered and checked your proposed names, click Next tab to proceed to the owner details section.

  7. 7

    Enter Owner Information

    On the "Owner" tab, fill in the personal details of the business owner:

    • Name and Surname
    • Gender (select from dropdown)
    • Date of birth
    • Nationality (select from dropdown)

    Upload a scanned copy of your National ID document.

  8. 8

    Enter Contact & Address Details

    Still on the "Owner" tab, provide your contact and address information:

    • Address — Your physical address
    • Municipality — Select from the dropdown
    • Google Maps reference — A link or reference for your location (optional)
    • Phone number
    • Email address
  9. 9

    Validate and Submit

    Click Validate the form to check that all required fields are completed correctly. If there are any errors, the system will highlight them so you can correct them. Once validated, proceed to submit your application and complete the digital payment.

Tip

You can save your progress and return later to complete the application. Your data is saved automatically as you fill in each section.

Warning

You cannot propose the same name twice. Each of your three proposed names must be different from the others. The system will alert you if duplicate names are detected.

6. After Submission

Once you have submitted and paid for your name reservation application, it enters a review process. Here is what happens next:

Application Submitted

Your application is received and a reference number is generated. You will receive a confirmation email with your reference number.

Day 0

Name Review

A government officer at the Registrar of Companies reviews your proposed name(s) for compliance with naming rules and regulations. They verify that the name is not identical or deceptively similar to an existing registered name.

Day 1–2

Decision

The officer approves or rejects each proposed name. If approved, one name will be confirmed as reserved for you. If all names are rejected, you will be notified with reasons and can submit a new application.

Day 2–3

Name Reservation Certificate

Once approved, your name reservation certificate is issued and available for download from the platform. You can then proceed with your full business registration.

Day 3
Tracking your application

You can track the status of your application at any time by logging into your account at services.businessregistrations.gov.ls. You will also receive email notifications when the status of your application changes.

What if my name is rejected?

If one or more of your proposed names are rejected, the officer will provide reasons for the rejection. Common reasons include:

  • The name is identical or too similar to an existing registered business
  • The name contains restricted or prohibited words
  • The name is misleading or offensive

You may submit a new name reservation application with different proposed names. Each new application requires a separate fee payment.
