What Is Name Reservation?

Before you can register a company in Lesotho, you must first reserve a unique business name. The Name Reservation service lets you propose up to three business names and check their availability against existing registered companies. Once the Registrar General's Office approves your chosen name, it is reserved exclusively for you for 60 days, during which you should complete your company registration.

ⓘ
Who should use this service? Any person who wishes to incorporate a Private Company, Public Company, or register another type of business entity in Lesotho. Both Basotho nationals and foreign nationals may apply.

Before You Start — What You Will Need

  • A device with internet access (computer, tablet, or smartphone)
  • A valid email address for receiving notifications
  • Your national ID document (scanned copy or photograph)
  • Three proposed business names ready (you may enter fewer, but three is recommended)
  • Personal details of the business owner (name, date of birth, address)
  • A phone number where you can be reached

Naming Rules & Guidelines

Choosing a suitable business name is crucial. The Registrar General will reject names that are already taken, offensive, or misleading. Here are the rules to follow:

Acceptable Names

  • Unique and distinguishable from existing registered names
  • Related to your business activity (e.g., "Maseru Fresh Produce (Pty) Ltd")
  • Creative combinations (e.g., "Thaba-Bosiu Holdings Ltd")
  • Owner's surname with descriptor (e.g., "Molapo Construction (Pty) Ltd")
  • Names ending with appropriate suffix: "(Pty) Ltd" for private, "Ltd" for public

Names That Will Be Rejected

  • Names already registered to another company
  • Names identical or too similar to government departments
  • Offensive, obscene, or misleading names
  • Names suggesting royal or government patronage without approval
  • Names using restricted words (e.g., "Bank", "Insurance") without a licence
✔
Pro tip: Prepare three different names ranked by preference. The system allows you to check availability instantly, but the Registrar may still reject a name during review. Having alternatives saves time.

How the Name Check Works

  1. Type your proposed business name in the text field.
  2. Click the corresponding Check button to search the registry.
  3. The system queries the Companies Registry database and tells you instantly whether the name is already taken or available.
  4. A green checkmark means the name is available; a red cross means it is already registered.
  5. If a name is taken, modify it and check again. Consider adding a geographic term (e.g., "Maseru", "Katse") or your industry keyword.
⚠
Duplicate names are blocked. The system will not let you submit two identical proposed names. Each of your three names must be different from the others. If you enter the same name in multiple fields, the system will alert you.
ⓘ
Sesotho or English? You may propose business names in either Sesotho or English. Names in both languages are accepted. For example, "Lehlaka la Thaba (Pty) Ltd" is perfectly valid.

Step 4: Validate & Submit Your Application

Once you have filled in all fields in the Owner tab, click the "Validate the form" button at the bottom. The system will check that:

  • All required fields are completed
  • At least one proposed name has been checked and is available
  • Your proposed names are each unique (not duplicates of each other)
  • Your national ID document has been uploaded

If validation passes, you will be able to submit your application. If there are errors, the system highlights the problematic fields in red — correct them and try again.

✔
After submission: You will receive a confirmation email with a reference number. Keep this number — you will need it to track your application status and for your subsequent company registration.

Processing Workflow — What Happens After You Submit

After you submit your application, here is what happens behind the scenes:

1

You Submit Your Application

Your completed form with proposed names, personal details, and national ID is submitted electronically via the portal.

2

Name Reservation Officer Reviews

A Name Reservation officer at the Registrar General's Office reviews your proposed names. They verify the name does not conflict with existing registrations, is not offensive, and complies with the Companies Act.

3

Decision: Approved or Returned

Approved: Your preferred name is reserved for 60 days. You receive an email confirmation with a Name Reservation Certificate.
Returned: If all names are rejected, you are notified by email with the reason. You can then submit new names.

4

Proceed to Company Registration

With your reserved name, you can now proceed to the full company registration service within the 60-day reservation period.

⚠
60-day deadline: If you do not complete your company registration within 60 days of name approval, the reservation expires and the name becomes available to others. You would need to apply again.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does the name reservation process take?
Typically 1 to 3 working days from the date of submission. The instant name check on the portal tells you if a name is already registered, but the Registrar General's Office still conducts a formal review before issuing the reservation certificate.
2. Can I reserve a name if I am not a Mosotho citizen?
Yes. Foreign nationals can reserve a business name in Lesotho. You will need to upload a valid passport instead of a national ID. Additional requirements may apply during the full company registration process for foreign-owned businesses.
3. What happens if all three of my proposed names are rejected?
If none of your proposed names are approved, you will receive an email notification with the reasons for rejection. You can then submit a new application with different names. There is no penalty for resubmitting.
4. Can I extend my 60-day name reservation?
Name reservations are valid for 60 days from the date of approval. If you need more time, you should contact the Registrar General's Office directly to enquire about extension possibilities. In most cases, you may simply re-apply if the name is still available.
5. Can I use Sesotho words in my business name?
Absolutely. Business names in Sesotho, English, or a combination of both are accepted. For example, "Mokorotlo Designs (Pty) Ltd" or "Lehlaka la Thaba Holdings Ltd" are both valid names. The name must still comply with all other naming rules.
6. Do I need to create an account on the portal before applying?
The system checks whether you are logged in before allowing submission. You should create an account or sign in before starting your application. Registration on the portal is free and only requires a valid email address.
7. What is the difference between a Private and Public company?
A Private company (Pty) Ltd has restrictions on share transfers and cannot offer shares to the public. It is the most common type for small and medium businesses in Lesotho. A Public company (Ltd) can offer shares to the public and is subject to additional regulatory requirements. Most entrepreneurs starting a new business should choose Private.
8. The system says my name "already exists" but I cannot find that company. Why?
The registry database includes all companies ever registered in Lesotho, including inactive or dissolved companies. A name that appears to be taken may belong to a company that is no longer operating but has not been formally deregistered. In such cases, you will need to choose a different name or contact the Registrar General for clarification.
9. Can I reserve more than one name at the same time?
Each application allows you to propose up to three names, but only one will be reserved (your top available choice). If you need to reserve names for multiple different businesses, you must submit separate applications for each.
10. I made a mistake in my application. Can I edit it after submission?
Once submitted, you cannot edit the application directly. If the Registrar finds an issue, they may return it to you for correction. If you notice an error immediately after submission, contact the Registrar General's Office with your reference number and explain the correction needed.
