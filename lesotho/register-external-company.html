Register an External Company

1. Overview

An external company (also called a foreign company) is a company incorporated outside the Kingdom of Lesotho that wishes to establish a place of business or conduct business activities within Lesotho. Under the Companies Act 2011, every external company must register with the Registrar of Companies before commencing business in Lesotho.

This service allows you to register an external company online through the OBFC eRegistrations platform. The process begins with a name reservation, followed by submission of the required registration documents and payment of applicable fees.

Who needs this registration?

Any company incorporated outside Lesotho that intends to establish a place of business, open a branch office, or carry on business activities within the Kingdom of Lesotho must complete this registration.

What you will receive

  • A Certificate of Registration as an external company in Lesotho
  • A unique company registration number
  • Legal authority to conduct business in the Kingdom of Lesotho

2. Requirements

Before starting your application, ensure you have the following documents and information ready. Having everything prepared will allow you to complete the application in a single session.

Eligibility

  • The company must be validly incorporated in its country of origin
  • The company must not be under liquidation or deregistration proceedings
  • An authorised representative or local agent must be appointed in Lesotho

Required documents

  • Certificate of incorporation from the country of origin (certified copy, apostilled or notarised)
  • Constitutional documents (memorandum and articles of association or equivalent charter documents)
  • List of directors with full names, nationalities, residential addresses, and identification details
  • Power of attorney or authorisation letter appointing a local agent or representative in Lesotho
  • Identification documents of directors and local representative (passport or national ID)
  • Proof of registered office address in Lesotho (lease agreement, utility bill, or similar)
  • Certificate of good standing from the country of origin (issued within the last 6 months)
Important

All documents originating from outside Lesotho must be either apostilled (if the country of origin is a party to the Hague Apostille Convention) or notarised and authenticated by the Lesotho embassy or consulate. Documents not in English must be accompanied by a certified English translation.

4. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to register your external company through the OBFC eRegistrations platform.

  1. 1

    Create an account

    Visit services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and click "Register" to create a new user account. You will need to provide your full name, a valid email address, and create a password.

    A verification email will be sent to confirm your account. Click the link in the email to activate your account.

  2. 2

    Log in and select the service

    Log in with your email and password. From the dashboard, locate and select "Register an external company" from the list of available services.

  3. 3

    Reserve a company name

    The first step in the registration process is name reservation. Enter your proposed company name for use in Lesotho. The system will check the name against existing registered names.

    You may propose up to three alternative names in case your first choice is not available. Once approved, the name is reserved for 60 days.

  4. 4

    Complete the application form

    Fill in all required fields in the online application form. This includes company details, director information, local representative details, and the registered office address in Lesotho. Fields marked with an asterisk (*) are mandatory.

    You can save your progress at any time and return to complete the form later.

  5. 5

    Upload supporting documents

    Upload all required documents as listed in the Requirements section. Ensure documents are clear, legible, and in an accepted format (PDF, JPG, or PNG, maximum 5 MB per file).

  6. 6

    Validate the form

    Click the "Validate the form" button to check that all required fields have been completed and all mandatory documents have been uploaded. The system will highlight any missing or incomplete information.

  7. 7

    Pay the fees

    Select your preferred payment provider and complete the payment for the applicable fees. A payment confirmation receipt will be generated and attached to your application.

  8. 8

    Submit the application

    Review your application one final time, then click "Submit". You will receive a confirmation email with your application reference number. Keep this number for tracking your application status.

Save your progress

You do not need to complete the entire application in one session. The system automatically saves your progress. You can log out and return at any time to continue where you left off.

5. Document Upload

The following guidelines apply when uploading documents to your application.

Accepted file formats

Format Extension Maximum Size
PDF documents .pdf 5 MB
JPEG images .jpg, .jpeg 5 MB
PNG images .png 5 MB

Tips for uploading documents

  • Scan documents at a minimum resolution of 300 DPI for clarity
  • Ensure all text in the document is legible and not cut off
  • If a document has multiple pages, combine them into a single PDF file
  • Colour scans are preferred over black-and-white for certified documents
  • Check that all stamps, signatures, and apostille seals are clearly visible
Warning

Documents that are blurry, incomplete, or do not meet authentication requirements (apostille / notarisation) will be rejected, and your application will be returned for correction. This may significantly delay the registration process.

6. After Submission

Once you submit your application, it will go through the following review process.

Application received

Your application is received and assigned a unique reference number. You will receive a confirmation email.

Day 0

Initial review

The Registrar's office conducts an initial review of your application and supporting documents for completeness.

Day 1 – 3

Detailed assessment

Your documents are verified for authenticity, and the proposed company name is confirmed against existing records.

Day 3 – 7

Decision

The Registrar approves or requests corrections to your application. You will be notified by email of the outcome.

Day 7 – 10

Certificate issued

Upon approval, your Certificate of Registration as an external company is issued and made available for download through the platform.

Upon approval

Tracking your application

You can track the status of your application at any time by logging into your account on the OBFC platform. Your dashboard will show the current status of each application.

If your application is returned

If the Registrar requires corrections or additional information, you will receive an email notification explaining what needs to be addressed. Log in to your account, make the requested changes, and resubmit your application. The review process will resume from where it left off.

Note

Processing times are estimates and may vary depending on the completeness of your application, the volume of applications being processed, and the time needed for document verification.

8. Legal Basis

The registration of external companies in Lesotho is governed by the following legislation:

  • Companies Act 2011 — Part XVI (External Companies), which sets out the requirements for registration, the obligations of external companies, and the powers of the Registrar of Companies.
  • Companies Regulations — Prescribes the forms, fees, and procedural requirements for company registration.
  • Business Names Act — Governs the registration and use of business names in Lesotho.
Key legal requirements

Under the Companies Act 2011, an external company that carries on business in Lesotho without registration commits an offence. The Act also requires external companies to display their name and country of incorporation at every place of business in Lesotho, and to include this information on all official correspondence and documentation.

Penalties for non-compliance

Failure to register as an external company, or failure to comply with ongoing reporting obligations, may result in fines and other penalties as prescribed by the Companies Act 2011. Directors and officers of the company may be held personally liable for certain contraventions.
