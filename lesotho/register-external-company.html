Register an External Company

Complete guide for registering a company incorporated outside Lesotho that wishes to conduct business within the Kingdom of Lesotho.

Name Reservation Registration included
Online 100% digital process
businessregistrations.gov.ls Official portal

1. Overview

An external company (also known as a foreign company) is a company incorporated or registered outside the Kingdom of Lesotho that establishes a place of business or carries on business activities within Lesotho. Under the Companies Act 2011, such companies are required to register with the Registrar of Companies before commencing operations.

This manual walks you through every step of the online registration process on the Lesotho eRegistrations portal at businessregistrations.gov.ls.

Who needs this registration?
Any company incorporated in South Africa, Botswana, Eswatini, Mozambique, or any other country that intends to operate a branch, office, or carry on business within Lesotho must register as an external company.

What you will accomplish

  • Name Reservation — Reserve your external company's name for use in Lesotho
  • External Company Registration — Officially register your company to operate in the Kingdom

2. Before You Begin

Eligibility requirements

  • The company must be validly incorporated in its country of origin
  • The applicant must be authorised to act on behalf of the company (e.g., director, company secretary, or authorised agent)
  • The company must designate a local representative or registered agent in Lesotho

What you will need

  1. A user account on businessregistrations.gov.ls (register for free if you do not have one)
  2. Company details from the country of origin — name, registration number, date of incorporation, registered office address
  3. Details of directors and shareholders — full names, nationalities, passport/ID numbers, contact addresses
  4. Lesotho local address — where the company will conduct business (e.g., Maseru, Leribe, or another district)
  5. Company constitutional documents — certified copies of your Memorandum and Articles of Association or equivalent
  6. Certificate of Incorporation — from the country of origin, certified or apostilled
  7. Board resolution — authorising registration in Lesotho and appointing a local representative
  8. Valid email address — for receiving notifications and your registration certificate
Tip
Gather all documents before starting the application. You can save your progress as a draft and return later, but having everything ready will speed up the process considerably.

Section C: Directors, Shareholders & Local Representative

You will need to provide details for the company's directors, shareholders, and a designated local representative in Lesotho.

Directors

For each director, provide:

  • Full name (first name, middle names, last name)
  • Nationality and passport or national ID number
  • Physical and postal address
  • Date of appointment

Shareholders / Members

For each shareholder or member, provide:

  • Full name or company name (if the shareholder is a legal entity)
  • Type of person: Natural (individual) or Legal (company)
  • Nationality or country of incorporation
  • Number and type of shares held

Local representative in Lesotho

Every external company must appoint a person resident in Lesotho who is authorised to accept service of process and notices on behalf of the company. Provide:

  • Full name of the local representative
  • Physical address in Lesotho
  • Contact telephone number and email address
  • Relationship to the company (e.g., director, lawyer, accountant, authorised agent)
Important
The local representative must be a natural person with a physical address in Lesotho. A P.O. Box alone is not sufficient. This person will receive all legal notices on behalf of the company.

6. Required Documents

Upload the following documents as part of your application. Accepted file formats are PDF, JPG, and PNG. Each file must not exceed 5 MB.

  • 📄
    Certificate of Incorporation (from country of origin)
    A certified or apostilled copy of the company's certificate of incorporation from the foreign jurisdiction.
    Required
  • 📄
    Memorandum and Articles of Association
    Or equivalent constitutional documents (e.g., company charter, bylaws). Must be certified.
    Required
  • 📄
    Board Resolution
    Resolution authorising registration in Lesotho and appointment of a local representative. Must be signed by directors.
    Required
  • 📄
    Passport / National ID of Directors
    Clear copies of identification documents for each listed director.
    Required
  • 📄
    Proof of Lesotho Business Address
    Lease agreement, utility bill, or letter from landlord confirming the physical address of the business in Lesotho.
    Required
  • 📄
    Power of Attorney (if applicable)
    If the applicant is an agent acting on behalf of the company, a notarised power of attorney is required.
    If applicable
  • 📄
    Good Standing Certificate
    A recent certificate of good standing or compliance from the registrar of the company's home country (issued within the last 3 months).
    Required
Document preparation tips
  • Documents in languages other than English or Sesotho must be accompanied by a certified translation.
  • Ensure all scans are clear, legible, and in colour where possible.
  • Apostilled documents are preferred for countries that are parties to the Hague Apostille Convention.

9. What Happens After Submission

After you submit your application, it enters the review workflow at the Companies Registry. Here is what to expect:

Stage Who handles it What happens Estimated time
1. Submission received System (automatic) Your application is logged and assigned a file number. You receive a confirmation email and receipt (if paid online). Immediate
2. Name availability check Registrar's Office An officer checks the proposed name against the existing register of companies in Lesotho to ensure it is unique and does not conflict with existing names. 1–2 business days
3. Document review Registrar's Office All uploaded documents are reviewed for completeness, authenticity, and compliance with the Companies Act 2011. 2–5 business days
4. Approval or query Registrar If everything is in order, the application is approved. If there are issues, you will receive a query notification via email with details of what needs correction. 1–3 business days
5. Certificate issued Registrar's Office Upon approval, a Certificate of Registration for the external company is generated and made available for download from the portal. 1 business day
Track your application
Log in to businessregistrations.gov.ls at any time to check the status of your application. You can also use the QR code provided at submission to access your file directly.

If your application is sent back

If the Registrar's Office sends your application back for corrections, you will receive an email notification explaining what needs to be amended. Log in to the portal, make the required changes, and resubmit. Common reasons for queries include:

  • Proposed name is too similar to an existing registered company
  • Documents are unclear, expired, or missing certifications
  • Incomplete director or shareholder information
  • Local representative details are missing or incomplete

10. Frequently Asked Questions

What is an external company under Lesotho law? +
An external company is any company that was incorporated or registered outside of Lesotho but intends to carry on business activities within the Kingdom. Under the Companies Act 2011, such companies must register with the Registrar of Companies before commencing operations. This applies whether the company is opening a branch office, a subsidiary, or simply conducting regular business transactions in Lesotho.
How long does the registration process take? +
The typical turnaround time is 5 to 10 business days from submission to certificate issuance, assuming all documents are in order and the name is available. If corrections are required, the timeline will be extended depending on how quickly you respond to queries.
Can I use the same company name as in my home country? +
You may propose the same name, but it must not conflict with an existing company already registered in Lesotho. It is common practice to add "(Lesotho)" to distinguish the local registration. For example, if your South African company is "Matla Construction (Pty) Ltd", you might register as "Matla Construction (Lesotho) (Pty) Ltd" in Lesotho. The Registrar will advise if the name is available during the name reservation step.
What is the role of the local representative? +
The local representative is a person resident in Lesotho who is authorised to accept service of legal documents and official notices on behalf of the external company. This person must have a physical address in Lesotho (not a P.O. Box). They may be a director, lawyer, accountant, or any other person appointed by the company. This requirement ensures that the Lesotho authorities can communicate with the company through a local point of contact.
I am a South African company — do I need an apostille on my documents? +
South Africa is a signatory to the Hague Apostille Convention, so an apostille is the preferred form of authentication. You can obtain an apostille from the South African Department of International Relations and Cooperation (DIRCO). Alternatively, you may have your documents certified by the South African High Commission in Maseru. Documents certified by a South African notary public are also generally accepted.
Can I save my application as a draft and finish later? +
Yes. The eRegistrations platform automatically saves your progress. When you log back in, go to your dashboard and look for applications with status "Draft". Click on it to continue where you left off. Your draft will be stored until you submit or delete it.
How do I pay if I do not have online payment options? +
You can select the "Cash payment" option during the payment step. The system will generate a payment reference. Take this reference to the Companies Registry office at the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Business Development in Maseru. Pay the fee at the counter and present the receipt. Your application will be processed once payment is confirmed. Note: You may also pay through Mpesa or EcoCash mobile money if those payment providers are available on the platform.
What happens after I receive my external company registration certificate? +
After receiving your registration certificate, you should:
  • Apply for a Tax Identification Number (TIN) from the Lesotho Revenue Authority (LRA)
  • Obtain any trade licences required for your specific business activity from the relevant district council
  • Register for VAT if your turnover exceeds the threshold
  • Open a local bank account in Lesotho (Standard Lesotho Bank, Nedbank Lesotho, or First National Bank Lesotho)
  • Comply with annual filing obligations, including filing annual returns with the Companies Registry
What if my application is rejected? +
If your application is rejected, you will receive a notification explaining the reason. Common reasons include: the proposed name is reserved or identical to an existing company, the documents are not properly certified, or the company is not in good standing in its home country. You can correct the issues and submit a new application. The name reservation fee may need to be paid again if the original reservation expired.
Is the Lesotho Loti the same value as the South African Rand? +
Yes. The Lesotho Loti (LSL) is pegged at par to the South African Rand (ZAR) under the Common Monetary Area agreement. 1 LSL = 1 ZAR. Both currencies are legal tender in Lesotho, so you can pay in either Maloti or Rand at the same value.
