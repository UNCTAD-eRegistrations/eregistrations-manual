Verify Your Identity (KYC)

This manual guides you through the Know Your Customer (KYC) identity verification process on the Lesotho Business Registrations portal. Identity verification is required before you can register a business, apply for permits, or access other government services on the platform.

1. What is Identity Verification (KYC)?

Know Your Customer (KYC) is a mandatory process that confirms you are who you say you are. The Government of Lesotho requires all users of the Business Registrations portal to verify their identity before submitting any application.

The verification uses an automated system called Didit that checks your personal information against official government records. In most cases, the process takes just a few minutes.

ⓘ
Why is this required? Identity verification protects you against fraud and ensures the integrity of official business records. It is a one-time process — once verified, you do not need to repeat it for other services on the portal.

Who needs to verify?

  • Any individual who wants to register a business in Lesotho
  • Company directors and shareholders
  • Applicants for trade licenses, permits, and other business-related services
  • Foreign nationals seeking to do business in Lesotho

2. Before You Begin — What You Need

Requirement Details
Portal Account You must have a registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls. If you do not have one, create an account first.
National ID Number Your Lesotho National Identity Document number (for Basotho citizens) or passport number (for foreign nationals).
Date of Birth Your exact date of birth as it appears on your identity document.
Internet Connection A stable internet connection. The verification process involves a live identity check and requires a camera.
Device with Camera A smartphone, tablet, or computer with a working camera for the Didit facial verification.
Physical ID Document Keep your physical ID card or passport nearby. The Didit system may ask you to photograph it.
⚠
Important: The name on your portal account must match the name on your national ID exactly. If your account name is different (for example, a nickname or abbreviated name), please update it before starting verification.

6. Step 4: Start the Identity Verification

After entering your ID number and date of birth, click the blue "Start identity verification" button. This launches the automated Didit verification process.

⚠
Before clicking: Double-check that your ID number and date of birth are correct. Once verification starts, the system will cross-check these against government databases. Incorrect information will cause the verification to fail.

What happens when you click the button:

  1. The system sends your information to the Didit verification service
  2. A verification window or prompt will appear on your screen
  3. You may be asked to take a selfie photo using your device's camera
  4. You may be asked to photograph your physical ID document
  5. The system will compare your selfie with the photo on your ID
  6. Results are returned automatically, usually within 1-3 minutes

7. Step 5: Complete the Automated Verification (Didit)

The Didit verification system will guide you through a short process. Follow the on-screen instructions carefully.

Tips for a Successful Verification

  • Good lighting: Ensure your face is well-lit. Avoid backlight (do not sit with a window behind you).
  • Remove obstructions: Take off sunglasses, hats, or face coverings. The camera must see your full face clearly.
  • Hold still: Keep your device steady when taking the selfie. Blurry images may cause a failed verification.
  • Clean lens: Make sure your device camera lens is clean and not smudged.
  • ID document: If asked to photograph your ID, place it on a flat, dark surface. Ensure all four corners are visible and the text is readable.
  • Stable connection: Stay connected to the internet throughout the process. Do not close the browser tab.
⚠
Do not close the browser tab while the verification is in progress. Closing the tab may require you to restart the entire process.

11. Understanding Verification Statuses

Your verification can be in one of the following statuses at any time:

Status What It Means What You Should Do
Not Started You have not yet started the KYC process. Navigate to the Verify Your Identity service and begin the form.
Approved Your identity has been confirmed successfully. Nothing more needed. You can now use all portal services. Your dashboard will show a green verified badge.
In Review Your application is being reviewed by a government officer. Wait for the review to complete. Check your email for updates. This typically takes 1-3 business days.
Declined Your verification was not successful. Check the rejection reason displayed on the form. Correct any issues and try again, or contact support.
⚠
If your verification is declined: Common reasons include a mismatch between the name on your account and your ID, an unreadable ID photograph, or poor lighting during the selfie. Review the rejection reason carefully, correct the issue, and resubmit.

12. Identification Documents

The KYC service does not require you to upload documents separately. Instead, the Didit system captures your ID during the automated verification. However, you should have the following documents ready:

Document For Whom Details
Lesotho National ID Card Basotho citizens Your valid national identity card issued by the Ministry of Home Affairs. Ensure it is not expired and the photo is clear.
Passport Foreign nationals A valid passport from your country of nationality. Must have at least 6 months of validity remaining.
✔
Good news: No document uploads are required. The entire process is handled through the Didit automated verification system, which captures and checks your ID in real time.

13. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the verification process take?
The automated Didit verification typically takes 1-3 minutes. If a manual review is required, expect 1-3 business days. You will be notified by email when the review is complete.
Do I need to verify my identity more than once?
No. Identity verification is a one-time process. Once your status is "Approved," you do not need to repeat it for other services on the portal. Your verified status will remain active.
My verification was declined. What should I do?
Check the reason for decline on the verification page. Common causes include:
  • Name mismatch between your account and your ID document
  • Poor quality selfie or ID photo (bad lighting, blurry image)
  • Expired ID document
  • Incorrect ID number or date of birth
Correct the issue and try again. If the problem persists, contact the support office.
I am a foreign national. Can I verify my identity?
Yes. Foreign nationals can use their passport for verification. Enter your passport number in the ID number field and complete the Didit verification as normal. If the automated system cannot verify your passport, a manual review will be initiated.
My camera is not working. Can I still verify?
The Didit system requires a camera for facial verification. Try using a different device such as a smartphone with a working camera. If you cannot access any device with a camera, contact the One Stop Business Facilitation Centre for in-person assistance.
I changed my name after marriage. How do I verify?
Ensure your portal account name matches the name on your current, valid ID document. If you recently changed your name, update your national ID first, then update your account name on the portal before starting verification.
What personal data is collected during verification?
The system collects your first name, last name, ID number, date of birth, gender, and nationality. A facial photograph is captured for comparison with your ID document. This data is processed in accordance with the Government of Lesotho data protection regulations and is used solely for identity verification purposes.
The verification is stuck on "In Review." Who do I contact?
If your application has been "In Review" for more than 5 business days, contact the One Stop Business Facilitation Centre. Provide your full name, email address, and the date you submitted the verification.
Can someone else verify on my behalf?
No. Identity verification is personal and requires your live presence for the facial recognition step. No third party can complete this process on your behalf. You must use your own face and your own ID document.
I completed verification but my dashboard still shows "Not Verified."
Try refreshing the page or logging out and back in. If the status does not update within 24 hours, contact support. The automated bot that updates your verified status may need to be retriggered by an administrator.
