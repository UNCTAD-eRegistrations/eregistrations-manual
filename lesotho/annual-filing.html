User Manual

Annual Filing

Submit your company's annual financial statements and update company records with the Companies Registry. This service allows registered companies in Lesotho to fulfil their annual filing obligations electronically.

1. Overview

The Annual Filing service allows registered companies in the Kingdom of Lesotho to submit their annual financial statements and update company records electronically through the One Business Facilitation Centre (OBFC) eRegistrations platform.

This service covers two key registrations:

  • Annual Filing — Submit your company's financial statements for each fiscal year.
  • Update of Data — Update your company's contact information, address, and other details in the Companies Registry.
Key Benefits

Filing online eliminates the need to visit government offices in person. You can submit your annual statements, track the status of your filings, and update company information from anywhere at any time.

Processing Workflow

Once submitted, your annual filing is processed through the following steps:

1

Submission

You submit your annual filing online with the required financial statements attached.

2

Review Filing

An OBFC officer reviews your submission and verifies the uploaded financial statements. The officer may approve or return the filing for corrections.

3

Register in Companies Database

Once approved, the filing is automatically registered in the Companies database (automated bot process).

4

Register in Accounts Database

The filing is also registered in the Accounts database (automated bot process). Your filing is now complete.

2. Who Must File

All companies registered with the Lesotho Companies Registry are required to file annual returns. This obligation applies to:

Private companies (Pty Ltd)
Public companies (Ltd)
External (foreign) companies operating in Lesotho
Any other entity registered under the Companies Act
Important

You must be a registered user on the eRegistrations platform and linked to your company to submit an annual filing. If you are not yet registered, please create an account first at services.businessregistrations.gov.ls.

3. Requirements

5. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to complete your annual filing online:

Step 1

Log In to the Platform

Visit services.businessregistrations.gov.ls and log in with your registered email and password. If you do not have an account, you must register first.

Step 2

Select the Annual Filing Service

From your dashboard or the services catalogue, locate and select the Annual Filing service. The system will automatically load your company information and display any pending or completed filings.

Step 3

Review Your Company Information

The first tab, Company Information, displays your company’s current details pulled from the registry. This includes:

  • Company name, registration number, and legal type
  • Capital and expiration date
  • Business sector and activities
  • List of owners with names and ID numbers
  • Contact details (email, phone) and address (district, town, street)

If you need to update any contact or address information, you can modify the fields in this section and select "Yes" when asked if you want the data updated in the Companies Registry.

Tip

You can update your contact information (email, phone, address) separately without submitting a filing by clicking the "Update contact information" button.

Step 4

Proceed to Annual Filings Tab

Click the "Proceed to annual filing" button to navigate to the Annual Filings tab. This tab contains two sub-tabs:

  • Filings Due — Shows all annual filings that are pending for your company, with the year and due date for each.
  • Filings Completed — Shows previously completed filings with their filing dates and uploaded statements.
Step 5

Upload Financial Statements

For each year that has a filing due:

  1. Tick the "Upload statements" checkbox next to the relevant year.
  2. Click the file upload field and select your company's financial statements document (PDF format recommended).
  3. Repeat for each year you wish to file.
Important

The financial statements upload is required. Your filing cannot be submitted without attaching the statements for each year you are filing.

Step 6

Submit Your Filing

Once you have uploaded financial statements for all years you wish to file, click the "File your annual statements" button to submit your application. The system will validate your form before submission.

Step 7

Track Your Filing

After submission, you can track the status of your filing through your dashboard. You will be notified if the reviewing officer requires any corrections or additional information.

7. Deadlines & Penalties

Companies are required to file their annual returns by the due date assigned by the Companies Registry. Key points to note:

Aspect Details
Filing frequency Annual — once per fiscal year
Due date As indicated in your filings grid on the platform. Due dates are set by the Companies Registry based on your company's registration date.
Late filing Companies that fail to file by the due date may be subject to penalties under the Companies Act, 2011. Persistent non-compliance may result in the company being struck off the register.
Multiple years If you have filings due for multiple years, you can submit them all in a single session. Each year requires its own financial statements upload.
Warning

Failure to submit annual filings can lead to your company being deregistered. Ensure you file on time every year to maintain your company's good standing.

8. After Submission

Once you have submitted your annual filing, here is what happens:

1

Confirmation

You will receive a confirmation that your filing has been submitted successfully. The filing will appear in your application tracker on the platform.

2

Officer Review

An OBFC officer will review your filing and the uploaded financial statements. This review typically takes a few business days.

  • If approved: Your filing proceeds to automatic registration in the databases.
  • If returned: You will be notified and asked to make corrections. Check your application tracker and email for details on what needs to be corrected.
3

Automatic Registration

Once approved, the system automatically updates the Companies database and the Accounts database. No further action is required from you.

4

Completion

Your filing moves to the Filings Completed tab, showing the filing date and the uploaded statements. Your annual filing obligation for that year is now fulfilled.

Data Updates

If you also requested an update to your company's contact or address information, those changes will be reflected in the Companies Registry once the filing is processed.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I file for multiple years at once?

A: Yes. If you have filings due for multiple years, they will all appear in the Filings Due tab. You can upload financial statements for each year and submit them together in a single session.

Q: What file format should my financial statements be in?

A: PDF is the recommended format. The system accepts common document formats, but PDF ensures the best compatibility and preserves formatting.

Q: My filing was returned for corrections. What do I do?

A: Log in to the platform and check your application tracker. The reviewing officer will have indicated what corrections are needed. Make the required changes, re-upload any documents if necessary, and resubmit.

Q: Can I update my company's address without filing annual statements?

A: Yes. On the Company Information tab, update your address fields and click "Update contact information". This submits only the data update without an annual filing.

Q: I see "Filings Completed" but no "Filings Due". What does this mean?

A: This means your company is up to date with all annual filing obligations. No further action is required until the next filing period.

Q: How do I know if my filing has been approved?

A: You can track the status of your filing through the application tracker on the platform. Once approved and registered in the databases, the filing will move to the Filings Completed tab. You may also receive email notifications about status changes.

Q: What happens if a bot registration fails?

A: In rare cases, the automatic registration in the Companies or Accounts database may encounter an issue. If this happens, the filing is automatically routed to a system administrator for manual resolution. You do not need to take any action — the issue will be resolved by the OBFC team.
