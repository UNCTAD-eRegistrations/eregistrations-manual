What is Annual Filing?

Every company registered with the Lesotho Companies Registry is required to submit annual financial statements. This obligation ensures that the public record is kept up to date and that business activities in Lesotho remain transparent.

The Annual Filing service on eRegistrations allows you to:

  • View all filings that are due for your company
  • Upload your audited financial statements for each year
  • Optionally update your company contact information (email, phone, address)
  • Track the status of your filings (due, submitted, completed)

The service also provides an Update of Data registration, which lets you update your company details in the Companies Registry at the same time as your annual filing.

ⓘ

Who must file? All locally registered companies and external companies registered in Lesotho. This includes private companies (Pty Ltd), public companies, and partnerships registered under the Companies Act, 2011.

How to upload:

  1. In the "Filings Due" sub-tab, locate the year you want to file for.
  2. Tick the "Upload statements" checkbox next to that year.
  3. Click "Choose File" and select your financial statement document (PDF format recommended).
  4. The file name will appear next to the button once uploaded successfully.
  5. Repeat for each year that is due.
⚠

Important: Financial statements must be uploaded as a single file per year. Accepted formats include PDF, JPG, and PNG. The maximum file size is typically 10 MB. Make sure your statements are complete, signed, and include the balance sheet, income statement, and notes to accounts.

💡

Filings Completed sub-tab: This read-only view shows all years for which statements have already been filed. You can view the filing date and download previously submitted documents for your records.

⚠

Companies registered this year: If your company was registered within the current calendar year, you will see a note indicating that no filing is due for the current year. Your first filing will be due in the following year.

What Happens After You Submit

Your application goes through the following workflow:

You Submit the Filing Applicant

You complete the form, upload documents, and click "File your annual statements". The system generates a file number and your application enters the review queue.

Review Filing Officer Review

A Companies Registry officer reviews your submitted financial statements. They verify that the documents are complete, correctly formatted, and properly signed. The officer may approve the filing or send it back for corrections.

Register in COMPANIES Database Automated

Once approved, the system automatically updates the Companies database to record that the filing has been completed for the relevant year(s).

Register in ACCOUNTS Database Automated

The system also updates the Accounts database with the financial statement information, ensuring all records are synchronised.

Filing Complete

Your filing status changes to "Completed". You can verify this by logging back in and checking the "Filings Completed" sub-tab. The filing date will be recorded.

⚠

Sent back for corrections? If the officer finds issues with your submission, you will receive a notification (via email or in your dashboard) asking you to correct and re-submit. Common reasons include: missing pages in the financial statement, unsigned documents, or documents for the wrong financial year.

Costs & Fees

Annual filing through the eRegistrations portal is currently free of charge. There are no government fees for submitting your annual financial statements electronically.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. When is the annual filing deadline?
Annual filings are typically due by 31 March of the year following the financial year. For example, financial statements for the year ending 31 December 2025 are due by 31 March 2026. Check the "Due date" column in your filing grid for the exact deadline applicable to your company.
2. What happens if I miss the filing deadline?
If you miss the filing deadline, your filing will appear as overdue in the system. Companies with overdue filings may face penalties under the Companies Act, 2011, including potential de-registration. It is strongly recommended that you file on time. If you have multiple overdue years, file them in chronological order starting with the oldest year.
3. Can I file for multiple years at once?
Yes. The system shows all years with filings due. You can upload financial statements for multiple years in a single submission. Simply tick the "Upload statements" checkbox for each year and attach the corresponding financial statement file. Each year requires its own separate document.
4. What format should my financial statements be in?
PDF is the recommended format. The system also accepts JPG and PNG image files. Ensure your documents are legible, properly scanned (if from paper), and include all pages of your financial statements. The maximum file size is typically 10 MB per document. If your file is too large, consider compressing the PDF or reducing the image resolution.
5. Do I need an audited financial statement?
It depends on the size of your company. Under Lesotho company law, companies that exceed certain thresholds (in terms of revenue, assets, or number of employees) must have their financial statements audited by a registered auditor. Smaller companies may submit unaudited (compiled) statements. If you are unsure, consult your accountant or contact the Companies Registry at +266 2231 7860.
6. Can I update my company address without filing annual statements?
Yes. The service includes a separate "Update of Data" registration. You can update your company's email, phone number, district, town, and street address by modifying the fields on the "Company Information" tab and clicking "Update contact information" without uploading any financial statements.
7. My company was just registered this year. Do I need to file?
No. If your company was registered within the current calendar year, no filing is due for that year. The system will display a message indicating this. Your first annual filing will be due in the following year. For example, if you registered your company in June 2026, your first filing will be due by 31 March 2027 for the 2026 financial year.
8. What does "sent back for corrections" mean?
This means the reviewing officer at the Companies Registry found an issue with your submission. Common problems include: uploading the wrong year's statement, missing signatures, incomplete documents, or unclear scans. You will see a notification in your dashboard and may also receive an email. Log in, review the officer's comments, make the necessary corrections, and re-submit the filing.
9. I have multiple companies linked to my account. How do I file for each?
When you open the Annual Filing service, the system loads the company associated with your file number. If you manage multiple companies, you will see a grid showing all filings due across all your linked companies. Each row indicates the company name, year, and due date. Upload statements for each company-year combination as needed.
10. How long does it take for my filing to be processed?
After submission, a registry officer typically reviews the filing within 3 to 5 working days. If approved, the automated database updates occur immediately. If sent back for corrections, the processing time depends on how quickly you re-submit. You can track the status of your application from your eRegistrations dashboard at any time.

Tips for a Smooth Filing

  • Prepare your documents in advance. Have your financial statements ready in PDF format before starting the online process. This avoids timeouts.
  • Use a stable internet connection. File uploads require a reliable connection. If you are in a remote district (e.g., Mokhotlong or Qacha's Nek), consider using a Maseru-based internet cafe or LTA WiFi hotspot if your connection is unstable.
  • File before the deadline. The 31 March deadline applies to all companies. Filing early avoids last-minute system congestion.
  • Keep a copy of your submission. After submitting, note your file number and save a copy of the uploaded financial statements for your records.
  • Check your email regularly. The system may send notifications if your filing is sent back for corrections. Respond promptly to avoid delays.
  • Update your contact details. If your company has moved offices or changed phone numbers since the last filing, use the "Company Information" tab to update the registry.
  • Ensure the correct financial year. Each uploaded document should clearly indicate the financial year it covers. Mismatched years are a common reason for rejection.
