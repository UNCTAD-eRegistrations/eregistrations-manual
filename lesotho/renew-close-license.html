1. Service Overview

This online service allows you to renew or close (terminate) your existing business licences in Lesotho. It covers both:

  • Trading Licences — for retail, wholesale, hawking, and general trading businesses
  • Industrial Licences — for manufacturing, processing, and industrial operations

You can renew and close multiple licences in a single application. For example, if you own a textile factory in Maseru (industrial licence) and a shop in Maputsoe (trading licence), you can renew one and close the other in the same session.

Who can use this service? Any registered business owner in Lesotho whose trading or industrial licence is active or approaching expiry. You must have an eRegistrations account linked to your business.

2. Before You Start

Requirements

  • A registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls
  • Your Licence ID number (printed on your current licence certificate)
  • A computer or mobile phone with internet access
  • A valid payment method for any applicable fees

Important Timelines

Renew before expiry! You can renew a licence up to 90 days before its expiry date. If your licence has already expired, you may still renew it, but penalty fees will apply for every day past the expiry date.

The system automatically detects how many days remain until your licence expires and calculates the applicable renewal or penalty fees accordingly.

Estimated Processing Time

  • Renewal: 2–5 working days after payment and submission
  • Closure (Termination): 3–7 working days after submission

Step 3: Select Your Action (Renew or Close)

For each licence, tick one checkbox:

  • Renew — to extend the licence for another period. The new start and new expiry dates are calculated automatically.
  • Close — to permanently terminate the licence. This cannot be undone; you would need to apply for a new licence if you wish to resume operations.
Warning: Closing a licence is permanent. Once your termination request is approved, the licence is cancelled. If you have a bakery licence in Teyateyaneng and you close it, you will need to apply for a brand new licence to operate again.

4. How to Renew a Licence

When you tick the "Renew" checkbox for a licence, the system will:

  1. Check whether the licence is eligible for renewal (expiry within 90 days or already expired)
  2. Calculate the renewal fee based on the licence type and sector
  3. Add a penalty fee if the licence has already expired (charged per day late)
  4. Display the new start date and new expiry date (typically one year from the renewal date)

Renewal Fee Breakdown

The fee summary section separates industrial and trading licences:

  • Industrial Licences Fees: lists each industrial licence selected for renewal, its renewal fee, and any penalty
  • Trading Licences Fees: lists each trading licence selected for renewal, its renewal fee, and any penalty

Totals are displayed at the bottom of each section, including Renewal fee (total) and Penalty fee (total).

Example: 'Mme Nthabiseng in Leribe has two trading licences. One expires in 15 days (no penalty) and one expired 30 days ago. She ticks "Renew" for both. The first has only the standard renewal fee. The second includes a penalty fee for 30 days of late renewal. Both fees are summed in the total.

5. How to Close (Terminate) a Licence

When you tick the "Close" checkbox for a licence, the system will:

  1. Record the termination date as today
  2. Calculate any termination processing fee
  3. Add a penalty fee if the licence is already expired and has outstanding days

Termination Fee Breakdown

Similar to renewal, the fees are separated by licence type:

  • Industrial Licences Termination Fees: termination fee + penalty for each industrial licence being closed
  • Trading Licences Termination Fees: termination fee + penalty for each trading licence being closed
Note: Even when closing a licence, outstanding penalty fees for expired periods must still be paid. Closing a licence does not waive any penalties owed.

9. Required Documents

This service currently does not require you to upload any supporting documents. All licence information is retrieved automatically from the government database based on your account.

No paperwork needed! Unlike older paper-based processes, this online service pulls your licence data electronically. You do not need to bring or scan any certificates, ID cards, or letters. Simply log in and the system handles the rest.

However, it is good practice to keep the following readily available in case officers request additional information:

  • Your national ID or passport
  • Original licence certificate (for reference)
  • Proof of business address (utility bill or lease agreement)
  • Tax clearance certificate (if applicable)

10. Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: Can I renew some licences and close others in the same application?
Yes. The system allows you to tick "Renew" for some licences and "Close" for others in the same session. For example, you can renew your Maseru trading licence and close your Mafeteng industrial licence at the same time. The fees for all selected actions will be combined in a single payment.
Q2: My licence expired more than 90 days ago. Can I still renew it?
Yes, you can still renew an expired licence. However, penalty fees will be calculated for every day past the expiry date. The longer you wait, the higher the penalty. If your licence has been expired for a very long period, contact the Ministry of Trade directly for guidance.
Q3: My licence does not appear when I click "List all your licences." What should I do?
This may happen if your licence is registered under a different entity type or name. First, verify you selected the correct Entity type (Licence) in the dropdown. If the problem persists, contact the licensing office with your Licence ID number so they can verify your records in the system.
Q4: What is the difference between a Trading Licence and an Industrial Licence?
A Trading Licence covers buying and selling activities (retail, wholesale, hawking). An Industrial Licence covers manufacturing, processing, or industrial production. If you run a shop selling goods, you have a trading licence. If you operate a factory producing goods, you have an industrial licence. Some businesses may hold both.
Q5: How is the penalty fee calculated?
The penalty fee is calculated automatically based on the number of days your licence has been expired. The system computes the difference between the expiry date and today's date. Each day of delay incurs a charge. The total penalty is displayed in the fee summary before you proceed to payment, so there are no surprises.
Q6: Can I cancel a closure (termination) after submitting?
Once your termination request has been approved by the licensing authority, it cannot be reversed. However, if your application is still under review, you may contact the licensing office and request the application be sent back to you so you can modify it. Act quickly if you change your mind.
Q7: Do I need to visit a government office in person?
No. This is a fully online service. You can complete the entire process — from selecting licences to paying fees and receiving your updated certificate — from your computer or mobile phone. You do not need to visit Maseru or any district office.
Q8: How will I receive my renewed licence certificate?
After your renewal is approved and processed, the updated licence certificate is uploaded to your eRegistrations account. You can log in and download it as a PDF. You will receive an email notification when it is ready. You can print it for your records or display it at your business premises.
Q9: I have licences in different districts (e.g., Maseru and Berea). Can I handle them all here?
Yes. The system loads all licences linked to your account regardless of district. Whether your businesses are in Maseru, Berea, Leribe, Qacha's Nek, or any other district, they will all appear in the list. You can renew or close any combination in a single application.
Q10: What happens if my payment fails or is interrupted?
If your payment does not complete successfully, your application will not be submitted. You can log back in and retry the payment. No duplicate charges will be applied. If money was deducted but the system did not register payment, contact the licensing office with your transaction reference number for manual verification.
