User Manual

Renew or Close a Licence

Manage your existing trading and industrial licences online. Renew before they expire or close licences you no longer need — all in a single application from anywhere in Lesotho.

1. Service Overview

This online service allows you to renew or close (terminate) your existing business licences in Lesotho. It covers both:

  • Trading Licences — for retail, wholesale, hawking, and general trading businesses
  • Industrial Licences — for manufacturing, processing, and industrial operations

You can renew and close multiple licences in a single application. For example, if you own a textile factory in Maseru (industrial licence) and a shop in Maputsoe (trading licence), you can renew one and close the other in the same session.

Who can use this service?

Any registered business owner in Lesotho whose trading or industrial licence is active or approaching expiry. You must have an eRegistrations account linked to your business.

Four Registration Types

Depending on the type of licence and the action you choose, your application is processed under one of four registration categories:

Registration Licence Type Action
Renewal of Industrial Licence Industrial Renew
Renewal of Trading Licence Trading Renew
Termination of Industrial Licence Industrial Close
Termination of Trading Licence Trading Close

The system determines the correct registration automatically based on your selections. You do not need to choose a registration type yourself.

2. Before You Start

What You Need

  • A registered account on businessregistrations.gov.ls
  • Your Licence ID number (printed on your current licence certificate)
  • A computer or smartphone with internet access
  • A valid payment method for any applicable fees
Important Timeline

You can renew a licence up to 90 days before its expiry date. If your licence has already expired, you may still renew it, but penalty fees will apply for every day past the expiry date. The earlier you renew, the less you pay.

Estimated Processing Time

  • Renewal: 2–5 working days after payment and submission
  • Closure (Termination): 3–7 working days after submission

3. Renewal vs. Closure

This service lets you perform two distinct actions on your licences. Use the tabs below to understand the differences.

Renewing a Licence

Choose renewal to extend your licence for another period. When you tick the "Renew" checkbox for a licence, the system will:

  1. Check whether the licence is eligible for renewal (expiry within 90 days or already expired)
  2. Calculate the renewal fee based on the licence type and sector
  3. Add a penalty fee if the licence has already expired (charged per day late)
  4. Display the new start date and new expiry date (typically one year from the renewal date)

After approval, your updated licence certificate will be uploaded to your eRegistrations account for download.

Closing a Licence

Choose closure to permanently terminate a licence you no longer need. When you tick the "Close" checkbox, the system will:

  1. Record the termination date as today
  2. Calculate any termination processing fee
  3. Add a penalty fee if the licence is already expired and has outstanding days
This cannot be undone

Closing a licence is permanent. Once your termination request is approved, the licence is cancelled. If you have a bakery licence in Teyateyaneng and you close it, you will need to apply for a brand new licence to operate again.

Mix and match

You can renew some licences and close others in the same application. For example, renew your Maseru trading licence while closing your Mafeteng industrial licence. All fees are combined in a single payment.

4. Requirements

Account & Information Needed

  • Active eRegistrations account linked to your business entity
  • Know your entity type (Sole Proprietor, Partnership, or Company)
  • Valid email address (for status notifications)

Required Documents

No document uploads required

This service does not require you to upload any supporting documents. All licence information is retrieved automatically from the government database based on your account. Simply log in and the system handles the rest.

However, it is good practice to keep the following readily available in case officers request additional information:

  • Your national ID or passport
  • Original licence certificate (for reference)
  • Proof of business address (utility bill or lease agreement)
  • Tax clearance certificate (if applicable)

5. Fees & Penalties

Fees are calculated automatically based on your licence type, business sector, and the number of days past expiry (if any). All fees are in Lesotho Loti (LSL).

Fee Types

Fee Type Applies To Description
Renewal Processing Fee Trading & Industrial Standard fee to renew a licence for the next period. Amount depends on licence category and sector.
Termination Processing Fee Trading & Industrial Administrative fee for permanently closing a licence.
Penalty Fee (Late Renewal) Trading & Industrial Charged per day for each day the licence has been expired. Calculated automatically from the expiry date to today.
Penalty Fee (Termination) Trading & Industrial If you close an already-expired licence, penalty applies for the period between expiry and the termination date.

Fee Summary Sections

The form separates fees by licence type so you can see exactly what you owe:

Summary Section Contents
Industrial Licences Fees Renewal fee + Penalty fee for each industrial licence selected
Trading Licences Fees Renewal fee + Penalty fee for each trading licence selected
Termination Fees (Industrial) Termination fee + Penalty for each industrial licence being closed
Termination Fees (Trading) Termination fee + Penalty for each trading licence being closed
Example

’Mme Nthabiseng in Leribe has two trading licences. One expires in 15 days (no penalty) and one expired 30 days ago. She ticks "Renew" for both. The first has only the standard renewal fee. The second includes a penalty fee for 30 days of late renewal. Both fees are summed in the total.

6. Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to complete your licence renewal or closure:

  1. 1

    Log In to Your Account

    Go to businessregistrations.gov.ls and log in with your email and password. If you do not yet have an account, you must first register as a new user.

  2. 2

    Select the Service

    Navigate to the "Renew or close a license" service from the services catalogue. Click on it to open the application form.

  3. 3

    Choose Your Entity Type

    Select your entity type from the "Entity type (Licence)" dropdown. This tells the system which business records to search. Options include Sole Proprietor, Partnership, Company, and others.

  4. 4

    Load Your Licences

    Click the "List all your licences" button. The system will query the government database and load all trading and industrial licences registered under your business entity into a grid.

  5. 5

    Review Your Licences

    Each licence is displayed as a row in the grid showing its name, licence ID, type, sector, activity, expiry date, number of days left (or days late), and calculated fees. Review each licence carefully.

  6. 6

    Select Renew or Close

    For each licence, tick one checkbox: "Renew" to extend the licence, or "Close" to permanently terminate it. You can select different actions for different licences.

  7. 7

    Review Fee Summary

    Scroll down to see the fee breakdown separated by industrial and trading licences. The system shows renewal fees, termination fees, and any penalty fees. Verify the total amount is correct.

  8. 8

    Proceed to Payment

    Click the "Go to payment" button at the bottom of the form. You will be directed to the digital payment gateway where you can pay the total fees.

  9. 9

    Submit Your Application

    After payment, you will see a confirmation page. Enter your email address for status notifications, tick the acknowledgement checkbox confirming the information is correct, and click "Submit" to send your application for processing.

8. After Submission

Once you submit your application, it goes through the following workflow stages:

Application Submitted

You complete the form, pay fees, and submit the application online. A reference number is generated.

Day 0 — Applicant

Review

A licensing officer reviews your application for correctness. They may send it back to you if there are issues that need correction.

Day 1–2 — Government Officer

Approval

A senior officer gives final approval for the renewal or termination of your selected licences.

Day 2–4 — Senior Officer

Licence Database Update

The system automatically updates the licence database with new dates (renewal) or closure status (termination).

Automated — LICENCES update bot

Certificate Upload

Your updated licence certificate is generated and uploaded to your eRegistrations account. You can download it as a PDF.

Automated — LICENCES upload bot
Track your progress

You can log in at any time to see which stage your application is at. You will also receive email notifications at key stages if you provided your email address during submission.

Application sent back?

If the reviewing officer finds an issue (e.g., your licence details do not match records), the application will be sent back to you with a note explaining what needs to be corrected. You can then edit and resubmit at no additional cost.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I renew some licences and close others in the same application?

Yes. The system allows you to tick "Renew" for some licences and "Close" for others in the same session. The fees for all selected actions will be combined in a single payment.

My licence expired more than 90 days ago. Can I still renew it?

Yes, you can still renew an expired licence. However, penalty fees will be calculated for every day past the expiry date. The longer you wait, the higher the penalty. If your licence has been expired for a very long period, contact the Ministry of Trade directly for guidance.

My licence does not appear when I click "List all your licences." What should I do?

This may happen if your licence is registered under a different entity type or name. First, verify you selected the correct Entity type (Licence) in the dropdown. If the problem persists, contact the licensing office with your Licence ID number so they can verify your records in the system.

What is the difference between a Trading Licence and an Industrial Licence?

A Trading Licence covers buying and selling activities (retail, wholesale, hawking). An Industrial Licence covers manufacturing, processing, or industrial production. If you run a shop selling goods, you have a trading licence. If you operate a factory producing goods, you have an industrial licence. Some businesses may hold both.

How is the penalty fee calculated?

The penalty fee is calculated automatically based on the number of days your licence has been expired. The system computes the difference between the expiry date and today's date. Each day of delay incurs a charge. The total penalty is displayed in the fee summary before you proceed to payment.

Can I cancel a closure (termination) after submitting?

Once your termination request has been approved by the licensing authority, it cannot be reversed. However, if your application is still under review, you may contact the licensing office and request the application be sent back to you so you can modify it. Act quickly if you change your mind.

Do I need to visit a government office in person?

No. This is a fully online service. You can complete the entire process — from selecting licences to paying fees and receiving your updated certificate — from your computer or mobile phone. You do not need to visit Maseru or any district office.

How will I receive my renewed licence certificate?

After your renewal is approved and processed, the updated licence certificate is automatically uploaded to your eRegistrations account. You can log in and download it as a PDF. You will receive an email notification when it is ready.

I have licences in different districts. Can I handle them all here?

Yes. The system loads all licences linked to your account regardless of district. Whether your businesses are in Maseru, Berea, Leribe, Qacha's Nek, or any other district, they will all appear in the list.

What happens if my payment fails or is interrupted?

If your payment does not complete successfully, your application will not be submitted. You can log back in and retry the payment. No duplicate charges will be applied. If money was deducted but the system did not register payment, contact the licensing office with your transaction reference number for manual verification.
